# EIKEN 3 (3級) - Reference Profile

**Source:** 2025 EIKEN Test Papers (1st, 2nd, 3rd Sessions)  
**Last Verified:** 2026-03-23  
**Status:** ✅ **Active Reference** (Use for all Eiken 3 content creation)

---

## 1. CEFR Alignment & Role
- **Level:** **A2 (Early)**
- **Role:** The **"Experience & Opinion" Level**. Students describe **experiences** (Present Perfect), make **comparisons** (Superlatives), and discuss **real possibilities** (First Conditionals).

## 2. Carried Over from Eiken 4 (Review Only)
*These are assumed known. Do not focus on them as the primary learning goal.*
- **Grammar:** Present Simple, Past Simple, Future (will/going to), Basic Modals.
- **Vocab:** Past events, future plans, school, travel, hobbies, basic feelings.
- **Sentence Length:** 5-8 words.

## 3. NEW Grammar Focus (The Core)
*These are the **only** new structures introduced at this level.*

| Grammar Point | Function | Example from 2025 Papers |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Present Perfect** | **NEW:** Experiences, actions with present relevance | "I **haven't seen** Karen for a while." <br> "**Have you been** to the new zoo?" <br> "Recently, the park **has been** dirty." |
| **First Conditional** | **NEW:** Real future possibilities | "**If** you **have** time, **come**." <br> "**If** it's not morning, I **can meet** you." |
| **Superlatives / Comparatives** | **NEW:** Comparing things, preferences | "She became one of the **most famous** photographers." <br> "Which is **more interesting**?" <br> "The **best** way." |
| **Basic Passive Voice** | **NEW:** Focus on action, not actor | "The road **was closed**." <br> "It **is called** the Three Sisters Method." |
| **Reported Speech (Intro)** | **NEW:** Indirect quotes | "She **said that**..." <br> "He **told me**..." |

### ❌ **Strictly Excluded (Do Not Use)**
- **Past Perfect** (had + past participle)
- **Second/Third Conditionals** (If I were..., If I had known...)
- **Present Perfect Continuous** (have been + -ing)
- **Complex Modals** (should have, could have)
- **Complex Relative Clauses** (who, which, that - avoid unless very simple)

## 4. Vocabulary Characterization
*Focus on **experiences**, **opinions**, **comparisons**, and **abstract topics**.*
- **Experience Markers:** `ever`, `never`, `before`, `since`, `for`, `recently`, `lately`, `already`, `yet`.
- **Opinions & Feelings:** `think`, `believe`, `feel`, `agree`, `prefer`, `enjoy`, `interested`, `excited`, `surprised`, `disappointed`.
- **Comparisons:** `more`, `less`, `better`, `worse`, `best`, `worst`, `biggest`, `smallest`, `most`, `least`.
- **Abstract Topics:** `culture`, `history`, `environment`, `technology`, `education`, `travel`, `health`, `community`, `festival`, `tradition`.
- **Verbs (Present Perfect):** `have/has been`, `done`, `seen`, `gone`, `eaten`, `taken`, `got`, `made`, `said`, `known`, `thought`, `come`, `left`, `felt`, `become`, `found`, `given`, `met`, `kept`, `built`, `bought`, `taught`, `caught`, `brought`, `heard`, `understood`, `spent`, `sent`.
- **Verbs (Conditionals):** `if`, `when`, `can`, `may`, `might`, `will`, `would`, `should`.

## 5. Sentence Structure & Length
- **Length:** **6-10 words** average. (Some up to 12-14 for complex ideas).
- **Structure:** Complex sentences with `if`, `when`, `because`, `and`, `but`, `so`.
  - *Example:* "**If** you have time, **come** to the party." (8 words)
  - *Example:* "She **has been** to Japan **three times**." (7 words)
  - *Example:* "The road **was closed** **because** there was an accident." (9 words)
- **Complexity:** Can combine clauses with `if`, `when`, `because`, `and`, `but`, `so`.

## 6. Example Sentences (Direct from 2025 Tests)
*These are the exact patterns to emulate.*

**Present Perfect (Core Focus):**

> "**Have you been** there before?"

> "**If** you **have** pictures, **bring** them."

**Superlatives & Comparatives:**

> "Which subject is **more interesting**?"

**Basic Passive Voice:**
> "The road **was closed** yesterday."

**Reported Speech (Intro):**
> "She **said that**..."

> "**Can you help** me?"
> "**Do you like** eating vegetables?"

## 7. Allowed Patterns Only (Strict Positive Constraints)
*The AI must ONLY use these patterns. If a pattern is not listed below, it is FORBIDDEN.*

### ✅ **ALLOWED Verb Forms**
- **Present Simple:** All base verbs.
- **Past Simple:** All common irregulars (went, came, saw, ate, took, got, made, said, knew, thought, left, felt, became, found, gave, put, met, kept, built, bought, taught, caught, brought, heard, understood, spent, sent, wrote, read, drew, ran, swam, flew, drove, rode, slept, woke, drank).
- **Present Perfect:** `have/has + past participle` (been, done, gone, seen, eaten, taken, got, made, said, known, thought, come, left, felt, become, found, given, met, kept, built, bought, taught, caught, brought, heard, understood, spent, sent).
- **Future:** `will`, `going to`, `can`, `could`, `may`, `might`, `would`, `should`.
- **Conditional (Type 1):** `If + Present Simple, will/can/may + base verb`.
- **Comparatives/Superlatives:** `more`, `less`, `better`, `worse`, `best`, `worst`, `biggest`, `smallest`, `most`, `least`, `-er`, `-est`.
- **Passive (Basic):** `am/is/are/was/were + past participle`.
- **Reported Speech (Simple):** `said that`, `told me`, `asked if`.

### ✅ **ALLOWED Question Words**
- `What`, `Where`, `Who`, `When`, `Why`, `How`, `How long`, `How many`, `How often`, `Which`, `Did`, `Was`, `Were`, `Will`, `Can`, `Could`, `Have`, `Has`, `Do`, `Does`.

### ✅ **ALLOWED Sentence Structures**
- **Simple SVO:** "I have been to Japan."
- **Complex (If/When):** "If you have time, come."
- **Comparative:** "This is more interesting than that."
- **Passive:** "The road was closed."
- **Reported:** "She said that..."
- **Compound:** "I went to the park, and I played soccer."

### ❌ **FORBIDDEN (If seen, the output is INVALID)**
- **Past Perfect:** `had + past participle` (e.g., "I had gone", "She had seen").
- **Second/Third Conditionals:** `If I were...`, `If I had known...`.
- **Present Perfect Continuous:** `have been + -ing` (e.g., "I have been studying").
- **Complex Modals:** `should have`, `could have`, `would have`, `must have`.
- **Complex Relative Clauses:** `who`, `which`, `that` (unless very simple).
- **Passive (Complex):** `has been done`, `had been done`, `will be being done`.

## 8. AI Validation Checklist (Run Before Output)
*If ANY item is unchecked, the content is INVALID.*

- [ ] **Verb Check:** Does every verb match the "ALLOWED Verb Forms" list (Present, Past, Present Perfect, Future, Conditional Type 1)?
- [ ] **Tense Check:** Is there **NO** Past Perfect (`had + ed`)?
- [ ] **Conditional Check:** Are all conditionals **Type 1** only (`If + Present, will/can`)?
- [ ] **Question Check:** Are all questions using `What`, `Where`, `Who`, `When`, `Why`, `How`, `Have`, `Has`, `Did`, `Will`, `Can`?
- [ ] **Length Check:** Is every sentence **≤ 12 words** (average 8-10)?
- [ ] **Complexity Check:** Are compound/complex sentences using only `if`, `when`, `because`, `and`, `but`, `so`?
- [ ] **Topic Check:** Is the topic concrete (experiences, opinions, comparisons, future plans)?
