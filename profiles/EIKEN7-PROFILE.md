# EIKEN 7 (7級) - Reference Profile (Theoretical)

**Source:** Speculative based on CEFR Pre-A1, Japanese Elementary Curriculum (Grades 3-4), and linguistic analysis of English-Japanese differences.  
**Status:** ⚠️ **Theoretical / Provisional** (Pending official 2026 release)  
**Target:** Elementary Grades 3-4 (approx. 8-10 years old)

---

## 1. CEFR Alignment & Role
- **Level:** **Below CEFR** (Pre-A1 / Starter)
- **Role:** The **"Foundational Awareness" Level**. Students learn the **basic building blocks** of English, with a specific focus on **overcoming Japanese linguistic habits** (syntax, pronunciation, pronoun usage).
- **Context:** Immediate environment, family, school, basic needs.

## 2. Carried Over from "Zero" (Review Only)
*Assumed knowledge:* Basic greetings, numbers 1-10, colors, simple Japanese-English cognates.

## 3. NEW Grammar Focus (The Core)
*These are the **only** structures introduced at this level.*

| Grammar Point | Function | Example from Theory |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Present Simple (Be-Verb)** | Identity, Location, Description | "I **am** a student." <br> "She **is** happy." <br> "It **is** a cat." |
| **Present Simple (Action)** | Basic habits, facts | "I **eat** apples." <br> "He **likes** dogs." <br> "We **go** to school." |
| **Basic Wh- Questions** | Asking for identity, location | "**What** is this?" <br> "**Where** is the book?" <br> "**Who** is she?" |
| **Yes/No Questions** | Confirming facts | "**Do** you like pizza?" <br> "**Is** it red?" <br> "**Can** you swim?" |
| **Possessives** | Ownership | "My book." <br> "Her cat." <br> "Our school." |

### ❌ **Strictly Excluded (Do Not Use)**
- **Past/Future Tenses** (went, will go)
- **Present Continuous** (is eating) - *Maybe light exposure, but not core.*
- **Plurals** (cats, dogs) - *Introduce only as "s" sound, not as a grammar rule yet.*
- **Articles** (a/an/the) - *Introduce only as "a" for single items.*
- **Complex Sentences** (because, if, when)

## 4. Key English-Japanese Differences (Critical Focus)
*This section is **mandatory** for content creation to prevent "Japanese-English" errors.*

| Feature | English (Target) | Japanese (Source) | **Teaching Strategy** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Syntax Order** | **SVO** (Subject-Verb-Object) <br> "I eat an apple." | **SOV** (Subject-Object-Verb) <br> "私はりんごを食べる。" | **Drill SVO relentlessly.** <br> "Who? -> What? -> Action." <br> Visual: [I] [eat] [apple]. |
| **Pronouns** | **Essential** (I, you, he, she, it, we, they) | **Often omitted** (Subject implied) | **Never drop the subject.** <br> "Eat an apple" is WRONG. <br> "**I** eat an apple" is RIGHT. |
| **Final Consonants** | **Required** (cat, dog, red, stop) | **Open syllables** (ka-tsu, do-gu, re-da, su-to-pu) | **Exaggerate final sounds.** <br> "Ca-t" (not "ka-tsu"). <br> "Re-d" (not "re-da"). |
| **R vs. L** | **Distinct sounds** (right, left, light) | **One sound** (ら行) | **Minimal pairs practice.** <br> "Right" vs. "Light". <br> "Rice" vs. "Lice". |
| **V vs. B** | **Distinct sounds** (very, big) | **One sound** (バ行) | **Teeth on lip for /v/.** <br> "Very" (not "Berry"). |
| **Plurals** | **Marked** (cat**s**, dog**s**) | **Not marked** (neko, inu) | **Introduce "s" as "many".** <br> "One cat. Two cat**s**." |
| **Articles** | **a/an/the** (specific/general) | **No equivalent** | **Introduce "a" for "one".** <br> "A cat" (one cat). <br> Avoid "the" for now. |

## 5. Vocabulary Characterization
*Focus on **concrete**, **immediate**, **tangible** items.*
- **People:** I, you, he, she, it, we, they, mom, dad, teacher, friend.
- **Body:** head, eye, nose, mouth, hand, leg, foot.
- **Family:** mom, dad, sister, brother, baby.
- **School:** desk, chair, book, pen, pencil, teacher, student.
- **Colors:** red, blue, green, yellow, black, white.
- **Numbers:** 1-10 (one, two, three...).
- **Animals:** cat, dog, bird, fish, cow, pig.
- **Food:** apple, banana, rice, bread, water, milk.
- **Verbs:** be (am/is/are), eat, drink, go, like, have, see, hear, say.
- **Adjectives:** big, small, happy, sad, good, bad, hot, cold.

## 6. Sentence Structure & Length
- **Length:** **3-5 words** maximum.
- **Structure:** Simple SVO only.
  - *Example:* "I **eat** an apple." (4 words)
  - *Example:* "She **is** happy." (3 words)
  - *Example:* "It **is** a dog." (4 words)
- **Complexity:** **NO** compound or complex sentences. No conjunctions.

## 7. Example Sentences (Theoretical)
*These are the exact patterns to emulate.*

**Identity & Description (Be-Verb):**
> "I **am** a student."
> "She **is** my friend."
> "It **is** a cat."
> "We **are** happy."

**Basic Actions (Present Simple):**
> "I **eat** an apple."
> "He **likes** dogs."
> "We **go** to school."
> "They **have** books."

**Questions:**
> "**What** is this?"
> "**Where** is the book?"
> "**Who** is she?"
> "**Do** you like pizza?"
> "**Is** it red?"

**Pronoun & Order Drills:**
> "I **have** a pen." (Not "Have a pen.")
> "She **is** tall." (Not "Is tall.")
> "He **eats** rice." (Not "Eats rice.")

## 8. Allowed Patterns Only (Strict Positive Constraints)
*The AI must ONLY use these patterns. If a pattern is not listed below, it is FORBIDDEN.*

### ✅ **ALLOWED Verb Forms**
- **Present Simple (Be):** am, is, are.
- **Present Simple (Action):** eat, drink, go, like, have, see, hear, say, play, run, walk, sleep, wake, open, close, read, write, draw, color.
- **Modals:** can (ability).

### ✅ **ALLOWED Question Words**
- `What`, `Where`, `Who`, `Yes/No` (Do, Is, Can).

### ✅ **ALLOWED Sentence Structures**
- **Simple SVO:** "I eat an apple."
- **Simple Be:** "She is happy."
- **Simple Question:** "What is this?"

### ❌ **FORBIDDEN (If seen, the output is INVALID)**
- **Past/Future:** went, will, going to.
- **Continuous:** is eating, are playing.
- **Dropped Pronouns:** "Eat an apple." (Must be "I eat an apple.")
- **Japanese Order:** "Apple I eat." (Must be "I eat an apple.")
- **Missing Final Sounds:** "ca" (must be "cat"), "re" (must be "red").
- **Missing "s" for Plurals:** "two cat" (must be "two cats" - if introduced).
- **Missing "a/an":** "I have apple" (must be "I have an apple").

## 9. AI Validation Checklist (Run Before Output)
*If ANY item is unchecked, the content is INVALID.*

- [ ] **Pronoun Check:** Is the subject (I, you, he, she, it, we, they) present in **every** sentence?
- [ ] **Order Check:** Is the sentence **SVO** (Subject-Verb-Object)?
- [ ] **Length Check:** Is every sentence **≤ 5 words**?
- [ ] **Sound Check:** Are final consonants (t, d, s, k, p) clearly implied in the text?
- [ ] **Grammar Check:** Is there **NO** past tense, future tense, or continuous form?
- [ ] **Topic Check:** Is the topic concrete (family, school, colors, animals, food)?
