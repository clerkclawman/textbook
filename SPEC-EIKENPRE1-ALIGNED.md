## 2. Test Reality Summary â€” What Eiken Analysis Shows

### The Official Description vs. Test Reality Gap

| Official Description | Test Reality | Impact on Teaching |
|---------------------|--------------|-------------------|
| "Bridge to university" | **Discourse markers are THE critical skill** | Teach discourse patterns, not just "more advanced grammar" |
| "Academic situations" | Academic register + logical coherence > complex grammar | Focus on formal tone and explicit connections |
| "Complex ideas" | Discourse markers create coherence, not long sentences | Use short sentences connected with clear markers |

### Four Pillars of Pre-1 Success

#### 1. Discourse Markers (CRITICAL â€” THE #1 Tested Feature, MISSING from official description)

**What the data shows:**
- Pre-1 introduces discourse markers as a **core tested feature**
- Unlike lower levels where simple connectors suffice, Pre-1 requires explicit logical markers
- Primary markers: *moreover, however, nevertheless, consequently, therefore, thus*

**Four marker categories (tested explicitly):**
- **Addition:** moreover, furthermore, in addition, additionally
- **Contrast:** however, nevertheless, on the other hand, conversely
- **Consequence:** therefore, consequently, as a result, thus
- **Conclusion:** in conclusion, to summarise, in summary
- **Tier 1 Priority:** Discourse markers are THE most important feature

**Test evidence:**
- 2ç´š: Simple connectors (and, but, so) sufficient
- æº–1ç´š: Discourse markers tested explicitly in reading comprehension and writing
- Students without discourse markers = automatic failure on writing section

#### 2. Academic Register (Formal Tone Required)

**Elements of academic register:**
- Objective language (avoiding *I think*, *In my opinion* â€” use *Research suggests*, *Evidence indicates*)
- Nominalisation verbs â†’ nouns (*decide* â†’ *decision*, *argue* â†’ *argument*)
- Latinate vocabulary over phrasal verbs (*examine* vs. *look at*)
- Passive voice for objectivity (*It is argued that...*)

**Academic vs. Non-Academic:**
- Non-academic: "I think this problem is bad."
- Academic: "This issue presents significant challenges."

**Why it matters:** Pre-1 reading passages are academic papers, research abstracts, or policy documents. Students must match this tone.

#### 3. Inversion and Ellipsis (Secondary Features)

**Inversion (stylistic emphasis):**
- *Never have I seen such...*
- *Seldom do researchers consider...*
- *Not only is X true, but Y...*

**Usage:** Low frequency compared to discourse markers. Do NOT overemphasise.

**Ellipsis (omission of understood elements):**
- *The study examined X, Y, and Z.* (Z understood to follow same pattern)
- *Some participants agreed; others refused.* (second "to participate" omitted)

**Teaching priority:** Include when it appears in exam texts, but focus learning time on discourse markers.

#### 4. Complex Linking (Beyond Basic Conjunctions)

**Advanced linking phrases:**
- *Despite the fact that...* (more formal than *Although...*)
- *In light of...* (formal cause)
- *With regard to...*formal topic introduction)
- *In terms of...* (formal category specification)

These are **supplementary** to discourse markers, not replacements.

### The "Advanced Grammar" Trap

**Current curriculum gap:**
- Many Pre-1 resources overemphasise inversion (*Seldom do I see...*) and ellipsis
- These features occur infrequently in actual exams
- Students spend disproportionate time on low-yield grammar

**Evidence from analysis:**
- Discourse markers: ESSENTIAL, tested throughout reading and writing sections
- Inversion: Occasional rhetorical device, not core skill
- Ellipsis: Stylistic option, not requirement

**Teaching priority should reflect:**
1. Discourse markers (#1 priority â€” minimum 6-8 per story)
2. Academic register (#2 priority)
3. Complex linking (#3 priority)
4. Inversion (<5% of teaching time)
5. Ellipsis (<5% of teaching time)

### Sentence Length Requirements (TV-Readable)

| Metric | Target | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| **Average sentence length** | 10-15 words per sentence | **TV-readable with translations** |
| **Minimum sentence length** | 8 words | Avoid fragmentation |
| **Maximum sentence length** | 20 words | Strict limit for readability |
| **Sentence length variety** | 8-15 word range | Keep natural flow |
| **Percentage of sentences** (15+ words) | 20-30% | Occasional longer sentences only |

**Critical Constraint:** All sentences must be TV-readable with Japanese translations displayed simultaneously. This constraint applies to **all Eiken levels** (5ç´š through 1çº§).

**Note:** These targets are for individual sentences (lines 1-10), not total story word count. Each story contains exactly 20 lines.

### Sentence Length Strategy (Maintaining Grammar Complexity)

**How to maintain Pre-1 complexity without long sentences:**

1. **Use discourse markers with short sentences** instead of long embedding
   - *Long:* "Moreover, studies have demonstrated that climate change is accelerating due to greenhouse gas emissions." (15 words)
   - *Short:* "Moreover, climate change is accelerating. Greenhouse gas emissions drive this trend." (12 words total, 2 sentences)

2. **Separate clauses into distinct sentences**
   - *Long:* "However, whilst universities have proposed reforms, critics argue that insufficient consultation has occurred." (14 words)
   - *Short:* "Universities have proposed reforms. However, critics argue consultation was insufficient." (11 words total, 2 sentences)

3. **Use discourse markers to connect short sentences**
   - "The research design was robust. Therefore, the findings are reliable." (11 words total, 2 sentences)

4. **Express complex ideas in 2-3 short sentences**
   - Long: "Academic register requires formal language which avoids personal pronouns and maintains objectivity through passive constructions." (18 words)
   - Short: "Academic register requires formal language. It avoids personal pronouns. Passive constructions maintain objectivity." (14 words total, 3 sentences)

3. **Everyday conversation topics** â†’ Pre-1 requires academic/professional themes
