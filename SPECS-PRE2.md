## Grammar Specifications

### Required Grammar Points

#### All Basic Tenses (Review from Eiken 3)
- Present simple, continuous, perfect
- Past simple, continuous, perfect
- Future (will, going to)

#### Past Perfect (New at this level)
- Earlier actions in relation to other past actions
- "I had already finished my homework when my friend called"
- Common expressions: had never seen, had always wanted

#### Complex Sentences
- Compound sentences with "and", "but", "so", "because"
- Introduction to basic relative clauses (who, which, that)
- Dependent clauses: "Although", "While", "Since", "Because"

#### Advanced Modals (New)
- "Should have done" (past advice/unavoidable situations)
- "Must have done" (deduction/certainty about past)
- "Could have done" (possibility in past)
- "Should", "must", "have to" for present obligations

#### Passive Voice (Expansion)
- Present and past passive expanded
- More common usage: "is made in", "was built by", "is spoken in"

#### Tag Questions (New)
- "It's beautiful today, isn't it?"
- "You like music, don't you?"
- "She can speak English, can't she?"

### Grammar Progression (48 Stories)
- Stories 1-10: Review all Eiken 3 grammar (perfect tenses, conditionals)
- Stories 11-20: Past perfect introduction
- Stories 21-30: Complex sentences and dependent clauses
- Stories 31-40: Advanced modals
- Stories 41-48: Tag questions + comprehensive review

## Vocabulary Characteristics

### Word Types
- **Part-time job vocabulary:** part-time job, interview, schedule, payment, manager
- **School vocabulary:** exam, test results, difficult subject, homework, class schedule
- **Everyday activity vocabulary:** buy food, watch TV, do homework, prepare dinner
- **Social vocabulary:** friend, family, neighbor, colleague, classmate
- **Basic emotion words:** happy, sad, excited, nervous, worried, disappointed
- **Future planning vocabulary:** next week, next month, this summer, next year

### Vocabulary Complexity
- **2 syllables average:** Most words are 1-2 syllables
- **Transition from A2 â†’ B1:** Introducing B1 words gradually
- **Abstract concepts beginning:** simple abstract words
- **Still mostly concrete:** Most vocabulary is visualizable

### Common Topics
- Part-time jobs (restaurant job, convenience store, tutoring)
- School life (exams, difficult subjects, club activities)
- Future plans (university, career, travel)
- Everyday situations (shopping, cooking, daily routines)
- Family and friends activities
- Personal goals and aspirations
- Hobbies and interests

## Content Requirements

### Sentence Length
- **Moderate complexity:** 10-12 words average
- **Compound sentences common:** using "and", "but", "so", "because"
- **Basic dependent clauses:** starting to use "Although", "While", "Since"

### Line Count per Story
- **Standard:** 10-12 lines
- **Pattern:** 1 emoji + 1 English sentence + 1 Japanese translation

### Question Format
- **3 discussion questions:**
  - 1 personal experience question
  - 1 opinion question (What do you think about...?)
  - 1 hypothetical question (What would you do if...?) â€” begin introducing
- **Answers should be 2-3 sentences:** Full responses required
- **Opinion reasoning beginning:** Encourage opinions with simple reasons

## Special Considerations

### Transition from Eiken 3
- **Critical level:** Moving from A2 to B1 is challenging for many students
- **Grammar confidence:** Ensure students feel secure with new grammar points
- **Progressive difficulty:** Each story builds on previous grammar

### High School Context
- **Part-time jobs:** Very common for high school students in Japan
- **Exams and tests:** Relevant to high school experience
- **Future planning:** Students start thinking about university/career
- **Social situations:** Friendships, family activities, school events

### Speaking Test Preparation
- **6-minute interview:** Eiken Pre-2 has mandatory speaking test
- **Skills needed:** Describing experiences, expressing opinions, answering questions

## Grammar Check for Pre-2

Before creating content, verify:
- [ ] Past perfect used correctly (preceding past actions)
- [ ] Complex sentences with dependent clauses
- [ ] Modal perfects used where appropriate (should have, must have)
- [ ] Tag questions match main verb correctly
- [ ] Vocabulary is from B1 level (3,000-4,000 words)
- [ ] Sentences are 10-12 words average
- [ ] Questions require 2-3 sentence answers
- [ ] Topics are age-appropriate for high school students (16-17 years)
- [ ] Progressive difficulty across the 48 stories

---

## Speaking Test Considerations

Eiken Pre-2 has a 6-minute speaking interview with:
- Describing experiences
- Expressing opinions with reasons
- Answering questions about topics

### Students should practice:
- Describing experiences in past and present perfect
- Expressing opinions with simple reasoning ("I think... because...")
- Answering hypothetical questions (Type 1 and Type 2 conditionals)
- Using tag questions naturally in conversation

### Content preparation:
- Include opinion questions
- Include hypothetical questions
- Include experience questions using past perfect
