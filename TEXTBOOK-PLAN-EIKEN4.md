## 1. Overview

### Curriculum Summary
- **Total stories:** 48 (Weeks 1-48)
- **Format per story:** 20 lines (10 sentences + 10 discussion questions)
- **Sentence length:** 12-14 words average (TV-readable with Japanese translations displayed simultaneously)
- **ALL lines (1-20):** Emoji + English (12-14 words) + Japanese translation

**‚ö†Ô∏è CRITICAL DESIGN PRINCIPLE: NO PROGRESSION**

**All 48 stories are at the SAME Grade 4 difficulty level.** No progressive building to "mastery."

**Why No Progression:**
- Students can join the level at ANY week (Week 1, Week 25, Week 45, etc.)
- Students read stories in order starting from the week they join
- Week 1 must be the same level as Week 48
- Each story is self-contained

**Weekly Changes = Variety Only (NOT Progression):**
- Mixed weeks integrate all three grammar features (Past simple, Future with will, Comparatives)
- Focus weeks emphasise ONE grammar point (Past, Future, or Comparative)
- Different topics for variety (school, hobbies, family, travel, sports, etc.)
- NO "introductory phase" or "advanced phase"

**Every Single Story (Weeks 1-48) Must Have:**
- Past simple: 1-8 sentences (depending on story type)
- Future with will: 0-8 sentences (depending on story type)
- Comparatives: 0-8 sentences (depending on story type)
- Time expressions: 1-5 (Past/Future focus stories require 3-5)
- Sentence length: 12-14 words (max 20)
- Japanese translations: ALL 20 lines

This makes infinite content generation possible for a real skill ‚Äî any week can be a student's first or last at Grade 4 level.

### Story Structure

**Lines 1-10:** Story sentences (age-appropriate content about a theme: school, hobbies, family, etc.)  
**Lines 11-20:** Discussion questions (stand-alone personal opinion prompts about the theme)

### Grammar Requirements by Story Type

#### MIXED Stories (24 total)
**Purpose:** Integrated practice with all three grammar features

**Grammar distribution:**
- Past simple: 3-4 sentences
- Future with will: 2-3 sentences
- Comparatives: 2-3 sentences
- Total: 10 sentences
- Time expressions: Past (2), Future (1)

**Topic flexibility:**
- Any age-appropriate topic
- All three grammar types naturally integrated
- Good balance of time expressions

**Example structure:**
```
Lines 1-2:   Past simple (what happened)
Line 3:      Future with will (plan/goal)
Line 4:      Comparative (comparison)
Lines 5-10:  Mixed grammar with variety
```

#### Focus: Past Simple Stories (8 total)
**Purpose:** Focused practice on past tense narration

**Grammar distribution:**
- Past simple: 7-8 sentences (DOMINANT)
- Future with will: 1-2 sentences (MINOR)
- Comparatives: 0-1 sentences (MINIMAL)
- Time expressions (past): 3-5 REQUIRED
  - *yesterday, last week, last month, last year, ago, in 2015, in 2020*

**Topic requirements:**
- Past experiences (last holiday, past trip, last school year)
- Memories (childhood, family events)
- What happened in the past

**Example structure:**
```
Lines 1-4:   Past simple sentences (story of what happened)
Lines 5-6:   More past simple details
Line 7:      Future (1 sentence, minor)
Lines 8-10:  Past simple conclusion
```

#### Focus: Future with Will Stories (8 total)
**Purpose:** Focused practice on future plans and predictions

**Grammar distribution:**
- Future with will: 7-8 sentences (DOMINANT)
- Past simple: 1-2 sentences (MINOR)
- Comparatives: 0-1 sentences (MINIMAL)
- Time expressions (future): 3-5 REQUIRED
  - *tomorrow, next week, next month, next year, soon, in the future, someday*

**Topic requirements:**
- Future plans (what I will do)
- Goals and dreams (career, school)
- Predictions (weather, events)
- Promises and commitments

**Example structure:**
```
Lines 1-4:   Future with will sentences (plans and goals)
Lines 5-6:   More future details
Line 7:      Past (1 sentence, context)
Lines 8-10:  Future conclusion (what will happen next)
```

#### Focus: Comparatives Stories (8 total)
**Purpose:** Focused practice on comparison structures

**Grammar distribution:**
- Comparatives: 7-8 sentences (DOMINANT)
- Past simple: 1-2 sentences (MINOR)
- Future with will: 0-1 sentences (MINIMAL)
- Comparative structures (required):
  - *-er* adjectives: 2-3 instances (bigger, taller, faster)
  - *more + adjective*: 2-3 instances (more interesting, more beautiful)
  - *-est* adjectives: 1-2 instances (biggest, tallest)
  - *most + adjective*: 1-2 instances (most interesting, most beautiful)
  - *as...as* or *not as...as*: 1-2 instances

**Topic requirements:**
- Preferences (A is better than B)
- Descriptions (taller, bigger, more beautiful)
- Choices (which option is better)
- Opinions (what I like more)

**Example structure:**
```
Lines 1-3:   Comparatives (A is better than B)
Lines 4-5:   Comparative descriptions
Line 6:      Past or future (1 sentence, minor)
Lines 7-10:  Comparatives and opinions
```

## 5. Content Quality Guidelines

### Primary Priority: Good, Readable Content
- **Above all:** Stories should be engaging and well-written
- **Readability:** 12-14 word sentences ensure TV readability with translations
- **Friendly tone:** Encouraging, relatable, simple but not childish
- **Topic relevance:** Age-appropriate themes (school, hobbies, family, sports, travel, etc.)

### Secondary Priority: Humour and Engagement
**Use humour where appropriate to make content memorable:**

**Appropriate humour themes:**
- Funny mistakes while learning
- Playful family situations
- Relatable school moments
- Surprising discoveries
- Gentle situations students will recognise

**Humour principles:**
- Gentle wit that students will recognise
- Irony about everyday situations they understand
- Playful commentary students relate to
- **Avoid:** Sarcasm, cynicism, mocking, or unkind humor

**When to use humour:**
- Opening lines can grab attention
- Examples can be playful
- Conclusions can be encouraging
- **When to avoid:** Serious topics like sadness or disappointment

### Tertiary Priority: Seasonal References (OPTIONAL, Not Required)
**Seasonal content is OPTIONAL ‚Äî do not force it**

**If using seasonal references:**
- Mention seasonal events only when they naturally relate to the topic
- Ensure seasonal events appear in their correct month:
  - Cherry blossoms: March-April (Japan)
  - Christmas: December
  - Halloween: October
  - Setsubun/Tanabata/Obon: Correct months in Japan
- Don't force seasonal connections that feel artificial

**Better non-seasonal than weak seasonal:**
- ‚úÖ Excellent non-seasonal content about hobbies
- ‚ùå Weak seasonal reference about Christmas in August (forced and confusing)

### Topic Flexibility
**Stories can be about ANY age-appropriate topic:**
- School and learning
- Hobbies and interests
- Family and friends
- Sports and activities
- Travel and places
- Food and daily life
- Seasonal activities ( optional)

**Quality principle:** Theme flexibility allows creators to choose topics they can write well about
