**Original spec was right on grammar but the IMPLEMENTATION was too hard.** Eiken 7 stories created were too complex.

- Eiken 5 is quite easy

## 2. Grammar Priorities — Clarified (v2)

### Grammar Inventory (Grade 7 - ULTRA Simple)

| Grammar Feature | Forms Include | Stories Using This | Notes |
|-----------------|--------------|-------------------|-------|
| **Be-verb (Present)** | is, are, am | ALL 48 stories | **PRIMARY structure** |
| **Present Simple** | I like, She/He likes | ALL 48 stories | **Simple 3rd person only** |

**EXPLICITLY EXCLUDED (NO EXCEPTIONS):**

| Grammar Feature | Why EXCLUDE | Clarification |
|----------------|-------------|--------------|
| Present Continuous | TOO complex | Use Be-verb: "It is red" not "It is turning red" |
| Past Be-verb (was/were) | Too early | Not introduced yet |
| Past tense | Too early | Not introduced yet |
| Modals (can, could, should) | Too early | Not introduced yet |
| Complex Wh-questions | Too early | Only basic: What? Where? Who? |

**CRITICAL IMPLEMENTATION RULES (v2):**

1. **Use 1st person** in stories: "I like cats", NOT "She likes cats"
2. **Use Be-verb for descriptions**: "The cat is black", NOT "The cat has black fur"
3. **NO Present Continuous**: "I play", NOT "I am playing"
4. **Simple vocabulary only**: concrete words children can see and touch

## 3. Story Structure — Format Requirements (v2 - CLARIFIED)

### Content Format Requirements (Critical Clarification)

Each story contains 20 lines total:
- **Lines 1-10:** 10 STORY sentences with emoji + English + Japanese
- **Lines 11-20:** 10 DISCUSSION questions with English + Japanese (**NO EMOJIS on questions**)

**⚠️ CRITICAL IMPLEMENTATION RULES:**

**Lines 1-10 (Story sentences):** Emoji + English + Japanese (two lines each)
**Lines 11-20 (Questions):** English + Japanese ONLY (two lines each, **NO EMOJIS**)

**CORRECT Format:**

```
😊 Sentence 1 (5-8 words).
日本語の翻訳1。

😊 Sentence 2 (5-8 words).
日本語の翻訳2。

[... 8 more story sentences with emojis and Japanese translations ...]

What is your name?
あなたの名前は何ですか？

Do you like cats?
猫は好きですか？

[... 8 more questions WITHOUT emojis ...]
```

**❌ WRONG FORMAT (don't use):**

```
😊 Sentence 1. 日本語1.  (English and Japanese on same line)
❓ What is your name?  (Emoji on question - WRONG)
I like cats.  (NO emoji on story line - WRONG)
```

### Sentence Length Requirements (TV-Readable)

| Metric | Target | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| **Average sentence length** | 5-8 words | TV-readable |
| **Maximum sentence length** | 10 words | Strict limit |
| **Minimum sentence length** | 3 words | Avoid fragments |


**Key points:**
- Lines 1-10: Emoji on EVERY line, 1st person ("I have", "I like")

## 5. Common Implementation Mistakes to Avoid (v2)

1. **Using 3rd person** → Use 1st person: "I like cats", NOT "She likes cats"
2. **Emojis on question lines** → NO emojis on questions
3. **Missing emojis on story lines** → EVERY story line needs emoji
4. **English and Japanese on same line** → Must be separate lines
5. **Sentences too long** → Keep 5-8 words, max 10
6. **Too many new words** → 3-5 per story MAX
7. **Using Present Continuous** → NO Present Continuous in Grade 7
8. **Complex vocabulary** → Keep words at 1-2 syllables
