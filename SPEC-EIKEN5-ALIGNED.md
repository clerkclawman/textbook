### Three Pillars of Grade 5 Success

#### 1. Present Continuous (CRITICAL â€” The "Now" Tense)

**What the data shows:**
- 5ç´š tests present continuous more than present simple (36 vs. 30 occurrences)
- Focus on **actions happening NOW**
- Pattern: "I am + [verb]ing" / "He is + [verb]ing" / "They are + [verb]ing"

**Test evidence:**
- "I am playing tennis." (tested frequently)
- "She is reading a book."
- "What are you doing?" (core question pattern)

**Teaching priority:**
- "What is he doing?" (NOT "What does he do?") for current actions
- Distinguish present continuous vs. present simple (NOW vs. HABIT)
- Focus on immediate, observable actions

#### 2. Wh-Questions (TESTED HEAVILY)

**Tested patterns:**
- **What...?** â†’ "What are you doing?" / "What is this?"
- **Where...?** â†’ "Where do you live?" / "Where are you going?"
- **Who...?** â†’ "Who is he?" / "Who are they?"
- **When...?** â†’ "When do you get up?" / "When is it?"
- **Why...?** â†’ "Why are you happy?" / "Why do you like it?"
- **How...?** â†’ "How do you go to school?" / "How are you?"

**Evidence:** Wh-questions account for 40%+ of tested patterns in 5ç´š

**Teaching priority:**
- Question word + be-verb: "Are you...?" / "Is he...?"
- Question word + auxiliary: "Where do you...?" / "What are they...?"
- Questions about actions and states

#### 3. Be-Verb Questions (Foundation Skill)

**Tested heavily:**
- "Are you...?" / "Is he...?" / "Are they...?"
- "Are you a student?" (yes/no question)
- "Is this your book?"
- "Are you happy?" / "Is he tired?"

**Test evidence:** Be-verb questions are 25%+ of tested patterns

**Teaching priority:**
- Master "Are you...?" pattern
- Distinguish "Are you...?" vs "Do you...?"
- Use be-verb questions about states and descriptions

### Pattern Explanation

**Alternating Rule (Mixed + Focus Rotation):**
- Odd weeks (1,3,5,7...): MIXED (all three grammar types integrated)
- Even weeks (2,4,6,8...): FOCUS (rotating PC â†’ Wh â†’ Be-verb â†’ PC â†’ Wh â†’ Be-verb...)

**Focus Week Rotation:**
- Week 2, 8, 14, 20, 26, 32, 38, 44: Present continuous focus (8 weeks)
- Week 4, 10, 16, 22, 28, 34, 40, 46: Wh-questions focus (8 weeks)
- Week 6, 12, 18, 24, 30, 36, 42, 48: Be-verb questions focus (8 weeks)

**No Progressive Build-Up â€” Just Variety:**
- Mixed weeks provide comprehensive practice
- Focus weeks provide concentrated skill development
- Both types maintain Grade 5 level difficulty
- NO progression from "easy" to "hard" â€” alternating at same level
- EQUAL representation of all three focus types (8 weeks each)

### Grammar Mix Distribution

**In "Mixed Grammar Weeks" (24 stories):**
- Present continuous: 4-6 instances per story
- Wh-questions: 4-6 instances per story
- Be-verb questions: 3-5 instances per story
- All three grammar types integrated naturally

**In "Focus Weeks" (24 stories total):**
- Present continuous weeks: 8-10 PC instances (PC dominant)
- Wh-question weeks: 7-9 Wh instances (Wh dominant)
- Be-verb weeks: 6-8 Be instances (Be-verb dominant)

### Rationale

**Why 50% mixed weeks:**
- Prevents grammar fatigue (doing the same thing for weeks)
- Provides comprehensive practice in natural contexts
- Reflects Grade 5 exam reality (mix of all tested features)
- Avoids artificial isolation of grammar points

**Why 50% focus weeks:**
- Allows concentrated practice on specific tested features
- Mirrors how exams focus on specific patterns
- Provides variety while ensuring all grammar is covered
- Prevents content from feeling "all the same"

**Test Reality Alignment:**
- Eiken exams test all grammar features together
- Students need comfort with mixed grammar
- BUT specific features (present continuous, Wh-questions) ARE tested heavily
- Balance: Mix practice (50%) + feature focus (50%)

### Present Continuous Guidelines â€” Core Grammar

**Pattern:** Subject + be-verb (is/am/are) + [verb]ing

**Forms:**
- Singular subjects: "I am...", "He is...", "She is...", "It is..."
- Plural subjects: "You are...", "We are...", "They are..."

**Examples:**
- "I am playing tennis." (Singular + am + playing)
- "He is reading a book." (Singular + is + reading)
- "They are eating lunch." (Plural + are + eating)
- "What are you doing?" (Question form)

**Present Continuous vs. Present Simple (Teach Distinction):**
- **Present Continuous (NOW):** "I am playing NOW." (happening now)
- **Present Simple (HABIT):** "I play tennis ON WEEKENDS." (routine)

### Wh-Question Guidelines â€” Core Grammar

**Pattern:** Wh-word + be-verb/auxiliary + subject + main verb

**Examples:**
- **What...?**
  - "What are you doing?"
  - "What is this?"
  - "What is your name?"
  
- **Where...?**
  - "Where do you live?"
  - "Where is the school?"
  - "Where are you going?"
  
- **Who...?**
  - "Who is he?"
  - "Who is your teacher?"
  - "Who are they?"
  
- **When...?**
  - "When do you get up?"
  - "When is your birthday?"
  
- **How...?**
  - "How are you?"
  - "How do you go to school?"

### Be-Verb Question Guidelines â€” Core Grammar

**Pattern:** Be-verb + subject + complement

**Examples:**
- "Are you happy?"
- "Is he your brother?"
- "Are they students?"
- "Is this your book?"
- "Are you hungry?"

**Positive and Negative Answers:**
- Q: "Are you happy?" â†’ A: "Yes, I am." / "No, I'm not."
- Q: "Is he your brother?" â†’ A: "Yes, he is." / "No, he isn't."
