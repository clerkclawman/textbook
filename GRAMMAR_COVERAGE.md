## Essential Differences Covered So Far

### ✅ Subject Omission
- **Japanese:** Often drops subjects when understood
  - 食べる (eat) - no subject
- **English:** Always states subjects
  - I eat / He eats / They eat
- **Sections:** 1, 2, 3, 8, 9, 10, 11

### ✅ Singular/Plural Nouns
- **Japanese:** No plural marking
  - 猫 (cat) = one cat or many cats
- **English:** -s for plural
  - cat vs cats
- **Sections:** 4, 6, 7

### ✅ Subject-Verb Agreement
- **Japanese:** Verbs don't change for person or number
  - 食べる (eat) - same for I, you, he, she, we, they
- **English:** Verbs change for 3rd person singular
  - eat vs eats, is vs are
- **Sections:** 4, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, 11, 12

### ✅ Articles
- **Japanese:** No articles
  - 猫 (cat)
- **English:** a/an/the required
  - a cat, the cat
- **Sections:** 5

### ✅ Word Order
- **Japanese:** SOV (Subject-Object-Verb)
  - 私はリンゴを食べる (I apple eat)
- **English:** SVO (Subject-Verb-Object)
  - I eat an apple
- **Sections:** 1, 2, 3, 4, 12

### ✅ Pronouns
- **Japanese:** Different pronoun system
  - watashi, anata, kare, kanojo, sore, watashitachi, karera
- **English:** I, you, he, she, it, we, they
- **Sections:** 1, 2, 3, 7, 8, 9, 10, 11

### ✅ To Be Verb
- **Japanese:** Uses "da/desu" which works differently
  - 猫だ (it's a cat)
- **English:** am/is/are with adjectives
  - The cat is big / It is a cat
- **Sections:** 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, 11

### ✅ Present Continuous
- **Japanese:** Uses ～ている for ongoing actions
  - 食べている (eating)
- **English:** am/is/are + verb-ing
  - am eating / is eating / are eating
- **Sections:** 12

## Essential Differences NOT Yet Covered

### 🔴 High Priority (Brain Reset Required)

#### 1. Present Simple - Third Person Singular -s
- **Japanese:** Verbs don't change for person
  - 私は食べる (I eat)
  - 彼は食べる (he eats - same verb form)
- **English:** 3rd person singular takes -s
  - I eat / He eats
  - I like / He likes
  - I run / He runs
- **Why essential:** Japanese speakers constantly forget the -s
- **Proposed Section:** Present Simple Drill

#### 2. Past Simple - Regular and Irregular
- **Japanese:** Past tense formed differently
  - 食べた (ate) - regular past formation
  - 行った (went) - irregular past formation
- **English:** Past tense varies
  - Regular: played, cooked, washed
  - Irregular: ate, went, ran, saw
- **Why essential:** English has many irregular past forms
- **Proposed Section:** Past Simple Drill

#### 3. Negation - Don't/Doesn't/Didn't
- **Japanese:** Negative formed with verb ending
  - 食べない (don't eat)
  - 食べなかった (didn't eat)
- **English:** Auxiliary verb + not
  - I don't eat / He doesn't eat
  - I didn't eat / He didn't eat
- **Why essential:** Japanese speakers try to negate the verb directly
- **Proposed Section:** Negation Drill

#### 4. Question Formation - Do/Does/Did
- **Japanese:** Add "ka" at the end
  - 食べるか？ (eat?)
  - 食べたか？ (did eat?)
- **English:** Auxiliary verb + subject + verb
  - Do you eat? / Does he eat?
  - Did you eat? / Did he eat?
- **Why essential:** Japanese speakers forget the auxiliary verb
- **Proposed Section:** Question Formation Drill

#### 5. There is/There are - Existence
- **Japanese:** Uses aru/iru for existence
  - 猫がいる (there is a cat)
  - 犬がいる (there are dogs - same form)
- **English:** there + be (singular/plural)
  - There is a cat / There are cats
- **Why essential:** Japanese doesn't distinguish singular/plural for existence
- **Proposed Section:** There is/There are Drill

#### 6. Prepositions - In/On/At
- **Japanese:** Uses postpositions (particles after nouns)
  - 机の上に (on the desk - literally "desk's top at")
  - 部屋に (in the room - literally "room at")
  - 学校で (at school - literally "school at")
- **English:** Prepositions before nouns
  - on the desk / in the room / at school
- **Why essential:** Japanese speakers put particles after, English before
- **Proposed Section:** Prepositions Drill

#### 7. Present Simple vs Present Continuous
- **Japanese:** Different aspect system
  - 食べる (eat - general)
  - 食べている (eating - ongoing)
- **English:** Different tense forms
  - I eat (habitual) / I am eating (now)
  - He eats (habitual) / He is eating (now)
- **Why essential:** Japanese speakers confuse when to use which
- **Proposed Section:** Present Simple vs Continuous Drill

#### 8. Countable/Uncountable - Much/Many
- **Japanese:** Different quantifier system
  - 水が多い (much water - same form for countable)
  - リンゴが多い (many apples - same form)
- **English:** Different quantifiers
  - much water (uncountable)
  - many apples (countable)
- **Why essential:** Japanese doesn't distinguish countable/uncountable
- **Proposed Section:** Much/Many Drill

### 🟡 Medium Priority (Useful but Can Learn Naturally)

#### 9. Wh- Questions - What/Where/When/Who/Why/How
- **Japanese:** Question word at end
  - 何を食べる？ (what eat?)
  - どこに行く？ (where go?)
- **English:** Question word + auxiliary + subject + verb
  - What do you eat? / Where do you go?
- **Why useful:** Different word order for questions
- **Proposed Section:** Wh- Questions Drill

#### 10. Adjective Position - Before Noun vs Predicate
- **Japanese:** Same form for both
  - 大きい猫 (big cat)
  - 猫が大きい (cat is big)
- **English:** Different positions
  - a big cat / The cat is big
- **Why useful:** Adjectives work differently
- **Proposed Section:** Adjective Position Drill

## Key Principles

### 1. Focus on Differences
- Only drill patterns that don't exist in Japanese
- Skip patterns that are similar or can be learned naturally

### 2. Minimal Examples
- Keep sentences simple (SVO)
- Use concrete nouns and common verbs
- Avoid complex structures

### 3. Consistent Translation
- Maintain consistent Japanese translation patterns
- Show the English-Japanese difference clearly

### 4. Repetition
- Rotate through pronouns, nouns, verbs
- Ensure exposure to all forms
- Build muscle memory

### 5. Practical Focus
- Target "European level" understanding
- Don't aim for native-level perfection
- Reset Japanese brain bias
