# Eiken 4 (4級) - Content Specifications

## Level Overview

- **Target:** Junior High Intermediate (中学中級程度)
- **CEFR:** Below A1 / A1 early (Present simple, basic will, simple past introduction)
- **Vocabulary:** 600-1,200 words
- **Story Count:** 48 stories (or 20+ for current format)
- **Primary Focus:** Building on basics with past tense and basic future

---

## Grammar Specifications

### Required Grammar Points

#### Present Simple (Review)
- All persons mastered from Eiken 5
- Third person singular perfect
- Questions and negatives fluent

#### Past Simple (Core Focus)
- **Regular verbs:** play → played, cook → cooked, watch → watched
- **Irregular verbs (expanded set):**
  - go → went, eat → ate, have → had, do → did
  - make → made, see → saw, buy → bought, get → got
  - run → ran, swim → swam, fly → flew
- **"Be" verb past:** was / were
- Both affirmative, negative, and question forms
- **Time expressions:** yesterday, last week, last weekend, etc.

#### Future Forms
- **"Going to" (primary):** I am going to go / She is going to study
- **"Will" (basic introduction):** I will go tomorrow/next week
- Present continuous for future: I am meeting him tomorrow

#### Comparatives
- **Basic comparatives:** big → bigger, small → smaller, fast → faster
- **Irregular comparatives:** good → better, bad → worse
- "Than" constructions: He is taller than me

#### Basic Questions
- **Wh- questions:** What, Where, When, Who, Why, How
- More complex question structures for details

### Grammar Progression (48 Stories)
- Stories 1-12: Past simple regular mastery
- Stories 13-24: Past simple irregular expansion
- Stories 25-36: Future forms (going to) + basic will
- Stories 37-48: Comparatives + mixed tenses

## Vocabulary Characteristics

### Word Types
- **Expanded verbs:** go, come, eat, drink, make, see, buy, get, run, swim, fly, play, watch, listen, read, study, etc.
- **School vocabulary:** classroom, teacher, student, textbook, homework, test, exam
- **Daily life vocabulary:** house, room, kitchen, bedroom, bathroom, desk, chair, table
- **Basic adjectives:** big, small, tall, short, old, new, good, bad, happy, sad
- **Time expressions:** yesterday, today, tomorrow, last week, next week

### Vocabulary Complexity
- **1-2 syllables:** Most words should be short
- **Moderate expansion:** Moving beyond core beginner words
- **Still concrete:** Visualizable objects/actions
- **Introduction of slightly more complex concepts**

### Common Topics
- Daily routines (wake up, eat breakfast, go to school)
- Past activities (what did you do yesterday?)
- Future plans (what are you going to do next week?)
- School life (tests, homework, teachers)
- Family/friends (activities together)
- Basic hobbies (sports, music, reading)
- Food preferences (like, dislike, favorite)

---

## Content Requirements

### Sentence Length
- **Moderate:** 8-10 words average
- **Compound sentences allowed:** "I went to the park and played tennis"
- **No complex sentences:** No dependent clauses

### Line Count per Story
- **Standard:** 10-12 lines
- **Pattern:** 1 emoji + 1 English sentence + 1 Japanese translation

### Question Format
- **3 discussion questions:**
  - 1-2 wh- questions (What/Where/When did you...?)
  - 1 yes/no question
- **Past-focused questions:** Connect to story theme
- **Personal:** About student's experiences and plans

---

## Special Considerations

### Bridge Level
- **Connects Eiken 5 → Eiken 3:** Critical foundation level
- **Past tense introduction:** First major grammar expansion
- **Confidence building:** Ensure students feel successful with new tenses

### Narrative Mode
- **Storytelling begins:** Past tense allows narrative
- **Chronological order:** "First I..., then I..., finally I..."
- **Time markers:** Important to use: yesterday, last week, this morning

### Real-World Reading
- **Eiken spec mentions:** Real-world reading materials (signs, notices, messages)
- **Consider adding:** Some stories about understanding signs/messages

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Grammar
- ❌ Overusing "will" — focus on "going to"
- ❌ Present perfect — NOT appropriate for this level
- ❌ Complex conditionals — not yet
- ❌ Relative clauses — not yet
- ❌ Inconsistent past time markers

### Vocabulary
- ❌ Low-frequency words — stick to core 600-1,200
- ❌ Abstract concepts — still keep mostly concrete
- ❌ Academic words — not needed yet
- ❌ Too many irregular verbs — master common ones first

### Content
- ❌ Boring chronological lists — make stories interesting
- ❌ Too varied topics — focus on past/future themes
- ❌ Too complex narrative — keep it simple and chronological

## Grammar Check for Level 4

Before creating content, verify:
- [ ] Past simple regular verbs used correctly
- [ ] Past simple irregular verbs (common set) used correctly
- [ ] Was/were used appropriately
- [ ] Future forms emphasize "going to" over "will"
- [ ] Comparatives use correct than-structure
- [ ] Time markers used consistently (yesterday, next week)
- [ ] Wh- questions are detailed and grammatically correct
- [ ] Vocabulary is from core 600-1,200 words
- [ ] Sentences are 8-10 words average
- [ ] Narrative flows chronologically
