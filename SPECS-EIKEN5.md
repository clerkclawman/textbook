## Grammar Specifications

### Required Grammar Points

#### Present Simple (Be Verbs)
- Basic affirmative: I am / You are / He is / She is / It is / We are / They are
- Basic negative: I am not / You aren't / He isn't / She isn't / etc.
- Basic questions: Are you...? / Is he...? / Are they...?

#### Present Simple (Action Verbs)
- Basic affirmative: I play / She eats / They go / We study
- Third person singular: He runs / She walks / It eats
- Basic negative: I don't play / She doesn't eat / They don't go
- Basic questions: Do you play? / Does she eat? / Do they go?

#### Present Continuous
- Affirmative: I am playing / She is eating / They are running
- Questions: Are you playing? / Is she eating?
- Limited use â€” present simple is primary focus

#### Basic Past Simple
- Regular verbs: played / cooked / watched / walked
- Irregular verbs (limited set): went / ate / had / did
- Past of "be": was / were
- Focus on completed actions recently

#### Basic Future
- "Going to": I am going to go / She is going to eat
- Very limited "will": I will go tomorrow

### Grammar Progression (48 Stories)
- Stories 1-16: Present simple (be verbs) mastery
- Stories 17-32: Present simple (action verbs) + third person singular
- Stories 33-40: Present continuous introduction
- Stories 41-48: Basic past simple introduction

## Vocabulary Characteristics

### Word Types
- **Core high-frequency:** family members, basic objects, daily activities
- **Colors:** red, blue, green, yellow, white, black, orange, purple
- **Numbers:** 1-20, basic counting
- **Basic pronouns:** I, you, he, she, it, we, they
- **Basic determiners:** this, that, my, your, his, her
- **Prepositions:** in, on, at, with, to, from
- **Basic verbs:** be, have, do, go, come, eat, play, study, like, want

### Vocabulary Complexity
- **Very simple:** 1-2 syllable words
- **Concrete:** All words should be visualizable/real
- **High-frequency:** Focus on most common 300-600 English words
- **No academic, abstract, or specialized vocabulary**

### Common Topics
- Family (mother, father, brother, sister, grandfather, grandmother)
- School (teacher, student, classroom, school bag, books)
- Animals (dog, cat, bird, fish, rabbit)
- Food (apple, banana, bread, rice, milk, water)
- Colors (basic 8-10 colors)
- Numbers (1-20)
- Basic actions (run, walk, eat, play, study, sleep)

## Content Requirements

### Sentence Length
- **Very short:** 5-8 words maximum
- **Simple structure:** Subject-verb-object only
- **No compound sentences:** No "and" connecting complex clauses

### Line Count per Story
- **Standard:** 10-12 lines
- **Pattern:** 1 emoji + 1 English sentence + 1 Japanese translation

### Question Format
- **3 yes/no questions:** Basic "Do you...?" or "Is/Are...?" format
- **Very simple answers:** "Yes," "No" or 2-3 word answers
- **Personal:** Questions about student's life/family

---

## Special Considerations

### Age Appropriateness
- **Target audience:** Junior high beginners (12-15 years old)
- **Not too childish:** Topics should respect their age
- **Simple topics:** Family, school, hobbies, friends

### Confidence Building
- **Positive tone:** Encouraging, not critical
- **Repetition:** Key patterns repeated across stories
- **Success-oriented:** Content should make students feel capable

### Visual Support
- **Emojis help:** Use relevant emojis for every line
- **Concrete references:** All content should be visualizable
