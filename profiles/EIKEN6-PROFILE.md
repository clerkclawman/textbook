# EIKEN 6 (6級) - Reference Profile (Theoretical)

**Source:** Speculative based on CEFR Pre-A1, Japanese Elementary Curriculum (Grades 5-6), and linguistic progression from Eiken 7.  
**Status:** ⚠️ **Theoretical / Provisional** (Pending official 2026 release)  
**Target:** Elementary Grades 5-6 (approx. 10-12 years old)  
**Role:** The **"Time & Aspect Bridge" Level**. Connects the static "now" of Eiken 7 to the dynamic "past/future/continuous" of Eiken 5.

---

## 1. CEFR Alignment & Role
- **Level:** **Below CEFR** (Pre-A1, moving towards A1)
- **Role:** Students learn to express **aspect** (ongoing vs. habitual) and **grammar mechanics** (plurals, articles). **NO Past or Future tenses yet.**
- **Context:** Daily routines, hobbies, weather, immediate actions.

## 2. Carried Over from Eiken 7 (Review Only)
*These are assumed known.*
- **Grammar:** Present Simple (Be & Action), Basic Wh- questions, Yes/No questions.
- **Vocab:** Family, school, colors, numbers, animals, food.
- **Focus:** SVO order, pronoun usage.

## 3. NEW Grammar Focus (The Core)
*These are the **only** new structures introduced at this level.*

| Grammar Point | Function | Example from Theory |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Present Continuous** | **NEW:** Actions happening *now* (Aspect) | "I **am eating** an apple." <br> "She **is reading** a book." <br> "They **are playing** soccer." |
| **Plurals (Regular)** | **NEW:** Marking "more than one" | "One cat. Two **cats**." <br> "Three **dogs**." |
| **Articles (a/an)** | **NEW:** Indefinite singular | "I have **a** pen." <br> "It is **an** apple." |

### ❌ **Strictly Excluded (Do Not Use)**
- **Past Continuous** (was eating)
- **Future Perfect** (will have eaten)
- **Conditionals** (if, would)
- **Complex Past Irregulars** (brought, caught, thought - stick to high-frequency: went, saw, ate, had, did, got).
- **Passive Voice**

## 4. Key English-Japanese Differences (Critical Focus)
*Building on Eiken 7, adding new challenges for time and aspect.*

| Feature | English (Target) | Japanese (Source) | **Teaching Strategy** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Aspect (Continuous)** | **"-ing" = Now** <br> "I am eating." | **"-teiru" = Now/Habit** <br> "Tabeteiru" (eating/habit) | **Drill "NOW" vs. "ALWAYS".** <br> "I eat" (habit) vs. "I am eating" (now). |
| **Plurals** | **"-s" sound** (cats) | **No marker** (neko) | **Exaggerate "s/z" sound.** <br> "Cat-s" (not "neko-tachi"). |
| **Articles** | **"a/an" = One** | **No equivalent** | **"A" = One.** <br> "A cat" (one cat). |
| **Subject Dropping** | **Never drop subject** | **Commonly dropped** | **Pronoun Drill.** <br> "Eat an apple" -> **WRONG**. <br> "**I** eat an apple" -> **RIGHT**. |
| **Word Order (Time)** | **Time at End** (usually) | **Time at Start** | "I play soccer **now**." <br> (Not "Now I play soccer.") |

## 5. Vocabulary Characterization
*Focus on **daily life**, **hobbies**, **weather**, and **simple narratives**.*
- **Time:** today, now, morning, afternoon, evening, night, week, weekend. (**NO yesterday/tomorrow**).
- **Hobbies:** play soccer, read books, draw pictures, watch TV, listen to music, swim, run.
- **Weather:** sunny, cloudy, rainy, snowy, hot, cold, warm, cool.
- **Verbs (Present/Continuous):** eat, drink, run, play, read, write, sleep, watch, listen, go, come, have, like, see, hear, say, study, talk, walk, stop, start.
- **Adjectives:** big, small, happy, sad, tired, hungry, full, hot, cold, wet, dry, fun, boring.

## 6. Sentence Structure & Length
- **Length:** **4-7 words** maximum.
- **Structure:** SVO with time markers (now, today) or continuous forms. **NO past/future forms.**
  - *Example:* "I **am eating** an apple **now**." (6 words)
  - *Example:* "She **is reading** a book." (5 words)
  - *Example:* "They **are playing** soccer." (5 words)
- **Complexity:** Simple sentences only. No conjunctions (and, but, because) yet.

## 7. Example Sentences (Theoretical)
*These are the exact patterns to emulate.*

**Present Continuous (Now):**
> "I **am eating** lunch."
> "She **is reading** a book."
> "They **are playing** soccer."
> "It **is raining** now."

**Plurals & Articles:**
> "I have **a** pen."
> "She has **two cats**."
> "It is **an** apple."
> "I see **three dogs**."

**Questions:**
> "**What are** you **doing**?"
> "**Are** you **eating** lunch?"
> "**Where** is the book?"
> "**Do** you like soccer?"

## 8. Allowed Patterns Only (Strict Positive Constraints)
*The AI must ONLY use these patterns. If a pattern is not listed below, it is FORBIDDEN.*

### ✅ **ALLOWED Verb Forms**
- **Present Simple:** All base verbs.
- **Present Continuous:** am/is/are + [verb]-ing.
- **Modals:** can, may, must.

### ✅ **ALLOWED Question Words**
- `What`, `Where`, `Who`, `When`, `Why`, `How`, `Are`, `Is`, `Do`, `Does`, `Can`. (**NO "Did"**).

### ✅ **ALLOWED Sentence Structures**
- **Simple SVO:** "I eat an apple."
- **Continuous:** "I am eating an apple."
- **Past:** "I went to school."
- **Future:** "I am going to play."
- **Plural:** "I have two cats."

### ❌ **FORBIDDEN (If seen, the output is INVALID)**
- **Past Continuous:** was eating, were playing.
- **Past Simple:** went, saw, ate, had, did, got, came, left, stopped, started, played, watched, read, drew, swam, ran, slept. (**FORBIDDEN**).
- **Future Forms:** will, going to, shall. (**FORBIDDEN**).
- **Perfect Tenses:** have eaten, had gone.
- **Conditionals:** if, would, could (unless "can").
- **Conjunctions:** because, although, when (as connector), if.
- **Dropped Pronouns:** "Eat an apple." (Must be "I eat an apple.")
- **Japanese Order:** "Now I play." (Prefer "I play now.")

## 9. AI Validation Checklist (Run Before Output)
*If ANY item is unchecked, the content is INVALID.*

- [ ] **Pronoun Check:** Is the subject present in **every** sentence?
- [ ] **Tense Check:** Is there **NO** Past Simple or Future tense?
- [ ] **Length Check:** Is every sentence **≤ 7 words**?
- [ ] **Plural/Article Check:** Are plurals marked with "s" and singulars with "a/an"?
- [ ] **Order Check:** Is time usually at the **end** of the sentence?
- [ ] **Topic Check:** Is the topic concrete (hobbies, weather, daily routine, present actions)?
