# EIKEN Pre-1 (準1級) - Reference Profile

**Source:** 2025 EIKEN Test Papers (1st, 2nd, 3rd Sessions)  
**Last Verified:** 2026-03-23

---

## 1. CEFR Alignment & Role
- **Level:** **B2** (Upper Intermediate / Advanced)
- **Role:** The **"Critical Analysis & Nuanced Argument" Level**. Students move beyond *expressing* opinions to **critiquing**, **debating**, and **nuancing** arguments with sophisticated connectors, nominal clauses, and highly abstract vocabulary.

## 2. Carried Over from Eiken 2 (Review Only)
*These are assumed known. Do not focus on them as the primary learning goal.*
- **Grammar:** Type 2 Conditionals, Complex Passive (all tenses), Advanced Relative Clauses, Opinion Expression.
- **Vocab:** Abstract/philosophical (politics, philosophy, duty, meaning of life, democracy, government).
- **Sentence Length:** 14-20 words.

## 3. NEW Grammar Focus (The Core)
*These are the **only** new structures introduced at this level.*

| Grammar Point | Function | Example from 2025 Papers |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Advanced Connectors & Inversion (Light)** | **NEW:** `Nevertheless`, `rarely`, `little`, `however`, `not only... but also` | "Nevertheless, critics have concerns." <br> "The crew **never** made it." <br> "Her work got **little** attention." <br> "**Not only** did it fail, **but** it also caused harm." |
| **Nominal Clauses (Complex)** | **NEW:** `the fact that`, `the reason why`, `what`, `whether`, `how`, `why`, `when`, `where` as subjects/objects | "The **question of why** the crew never made it remains unanswered." <br> "The **mystery of what** happened to the Mary Celeste..." <br> "**Whether** they lived or died was uncertain." <br> "**How** the children were taught matters." |
| **Complex Noun Phrases** | **NEW:** Long, dense noun phrases with embedded clauses | "The **early 1930s** saw a severe drought..." <br> "The **issue of** insufficient teachers..." <br> "The **debate over** whether to implement..." |
| **Nuanced Opinion & Critique** | **NEW:** Expressing doubt, skepticism, partial agreement | "Critics **have concerns**." <br> "It was **discovered that** the program was insufficient." <br> "**Despite** government efforts, resistance persisted." <br> "It could **also be dangerous**." |
| **Abstract/Academic Vocabulary** | **NEW:** Perspectives, autonomy, management, culture, resolution, aggression, empowerment, rights, discrimination, systemic | `perspective`, `autonomy`, `management`, `culture`, `resolution`, `aggression`, `empowerment`, `rights`, `discrimination`, `systemic`, `framework`, `paradigm`, `methodology`, `hypothesis`, `evidence`, `data`, `analysis`, `interpretation`, `implication`, `consequence`. |

### ❌ **Strictly Excluded (Do Not Use)**
- **Subjunctive Mood** (I suggest that he be...) - *Not tested.*
- **Very Long Sentences** (>25 words) - *Keep under 22.*
- **Advanced Inversion** (Never have I seen...) - *Rare, avoid.*
- **C1-Level Nuance** (Advanced nominalization, complex passive perfect continuous) - *Not tested.*
- **Type 3 Conditionals:** **ALLOWED** for past hypotheses (If I had known..., I would have gone...), but not the core focus.

## 5. Sentence Structure & Length
- **Length:** **12-22 words** average. (Some up to 25 for complex ideas).
- **Structure:** Complex sentences with `the fact that`, `the reason why`, `what`, `whether`, `how`, `why`, `when`, `where`, `nevertheless`, `however`, `rarely`, `little`, `not only... but also`, `although`, `because`, `if`, `when`, `since`, `as`, `and`, `but`, `so`.
  - *Example:* "The **question of why** the crew never made it remains **unanswered**." (12 words)
  - *Example:* "**Nevertheless**, critics **have concerns** about the **data**." (9 words)
  - *Example:* "**How** the children were taught **matters** more than **what** they learned." (12 words)
- **Complexity:** Can combine multiple clauses with nominal clauses and advanced connectors.

## 6. Example Sentences (Direct from 2025 Tests)
*These are the exact patterns to emulate.*

**Advanced Connectors & Inversion (Nuance):**
> "**Nevertheless**, critics have concerns."
> "The crew **never** made it to Santa Maria."
> "Her work got **little** attention for many years."
> "**Not only** did it fail, **but** it also caused harm."
> "**However**, the early 1930s saw a severe drought."
> "**Rarely** do we see such a phenomenon."

**Nominal Clauses (Complex):**
> "The **question of why** the crew never made it remains unanswered."
> "The **mystery of what** happened to the Mary Celeste..."
> "**Whether** they lived or died was uncertain."
> "**How** the children were taught **matters**."
> "**What** one biologist said about induced resistance..."
> "**The reason why** the program failed is unclear."

**Complex Noun Phrases:**
> "The **early 1930s** saw a severe drought that led to huge numbers of emus."
> "The **issue of** insufficient teachers was a major problem."
> "The **debate over** whether to implement the policy continued."
> "The **question of** how to balance economic growth and environmental protection."
> "The **challenge of** addressing climate change while maintaining economic growth."

**Nuanced Opinion & Critique:**
> "Critics **have concerns** about the data."
> "It was **discovered that** the program was insufficient."
> "**Despite** government efforts, resistance persisted."
> "It could **also be dangerous** to proceed without further research."
> "The **evidence suggests** that the theory is flawed."
> "It is **plausible** that the hypothesis is correct, but **not proven**."

**Abstract/Academic Vocabulary:**
> "The **perspective** of the community is crucial."
> "Students need **autonomy** in their learning."
> "Effective **management** requires strong leadership."
> "The **culture** of the organization influences behavior."
> "The **resolution** of the conflict took years."
> "The **aggression** displayed was unexpected."
> "**Empowerment** of marginalized groups is essential."
> "Human **rights** must be protected."
> "**Discrimination** based on race is unacceptable."
> "**Systemic** racism requires systemic solutions."

**Questions (Wh- & Yes/No):**
> "**What** is the **perspective** of the community?"
> "**How** does **autonomy** affect learning outcomes?"
> "**Why** is **effective management** important?"
> "**Whether** the policy will succeed is uncertain."
> "**How** can we balance economic growth and environmental protection?"

## 7. Allowed Patterns Only (Strict Positive Constraints)
*The AI must ONLY use these patterns. If a pattern is not listed below, it is FORBIDDEN.*

### ✅ **ALLOWED Verb Forms**
- **Present Simple:** All base verbs.
- **Past Simple:** All common irregulars.
- **Past Continuous:** `was/were + [verb]-ing`.
- **Past Perfect:** `had + past participle`.
- **Present Perfect:** `have/has + past participle`.
- **Future:** `will`, `going to`, `can`, `could`, `may`, `might`, `would`, `should`, `must`, `have to`, `need to`.
- **Conditional (Type 1, 2, & 3):** `If + Present, will/can` AND `If + Past, would/could/might` AND `If + Past Perfect, would have/could have/might have` (for past hypotheses).
- **Comparatives/Superlatives:** `more`, `less`, `better`, `worse`, `best`, `worst`, `biggest`, `smallest`, `most`, `least`, `-er`, `-est`.
- **Passive (All Tenses):** `am/is/are/was/were/has been/have been/will be/had been/could have been/would have been/might have been + past participle`.
- **Reported Speech (Complex):** `said that`, `told me`, `asked if`, `explained that`, `mentioned that`, `wondered`, `wanted to know`, `argued that`, `claimed that`, `suggested that`, `recommended that`, `questioned`, `doubted`, `skeptical`.
- **Relative Clauses (Advanced):** `who`, `which`, `that`, `whose`, `whom`, `where`, `when`, `at which`.
- **Nominal Clauses:** `the fact that`, `the reason why`, `what`, `whatever`, `whoever`, `whichever`, `whether`, `whether or not`, `how`, `why`, `when`, `where`.

### ✅ **ALLOWED Question Words**
- `What`, `Where`, `Who`, `When`, `Why`, `How`, `How long`, `How many`, `How often`, `Which`, `Did`, `Was`, `Were`, `Will`, `Can`, `Could`, `Have`, `Has`, `Do`, `Does`, `Should`, `Must`, `Have to`, `Need to`, `Would`, `Could`, `Might`, `Whether`.

### ✅ **ALLOWED Sentence Structures**
- **Simple SVO:** "I have been to Japan."
- **Complex (Nominal Clauses):** "The question of why the crew never made it remains unanswered."
- **Complex (Advanced Connectors):** "Nevertheless, critics have concerns."
- **Conditional (Type 1, 2, & 3):** "If you have time, come." / "If I had time, I would come." / "If I had known, I would have gone."
- **Passive (All Tenses):** "The road was closed." / "It has been limited." / "It had been done." / "It would have been done."
- **Reported (Complex):** "She said that he wanted to be an actor." / "He argued that the policy should change."
- **Compound:** "I went to the park, and I played soccer."

### ❌ **FORBIDDEN (If seen, the output is INVALID)**
- **Subjunctive Mood:** `If I were`, `I suggest that he be`.
- **Subjunctive Mood:** `If I were`, `I suggest that he be`.
- **Advanced Inversion:** `Never have I seen...`, `Seldom do we...` (unless very natural).
- **Very Long Sentences:** >25 words.
- **Slang/Colloquial:** Informal language, contractions (unless natural).

## 8. AI Validation Checklist (Run Before Output)
*If ANY item is unchecked, the content is INVALID.*

- [ ] **Verb Check:** Does every verb match the "ALLOWED Verb Forms" list (Present, Past, Past Continuous, Past Perfect, Present Perfect, Future, Passive (all tenses), Reported, Relative, Nominal)?
- [ ] **Tense Check:** Is there **NO** Type 3 Conditionals or Subjunctive (unless necessary)?
- [ ] **Conditional Check:** Are conditionals **Type 1 or 2** only (no Type 3)?
- [ ] **Question Check:** Are all questions using `What`, `Where`, `Who`, `When`, `Why`, `How`, `Have`, `Has`, `Did`, `Will`, `Can`, `Should`, `Must`, `Would`, `Could`, `Might`, `Whether`?
- [ ] **Length Check:** Is every sentence **≤ 22 words** (average 14-15)?
- [ ] **Complexity Check:** Are compound/complex sentences using `the fact that`, `the reason why`, `what`, `whether`, `how`, `why`, `when`, `where`, `nevertheless`, `however`, `rarely`, `little`, `not only... but also`, `although`, `because`, `if`, `when`, `since`, `as`, `and`, `but`, `so`?
