## 1. Overview

### Curriculum Summary
- **Total stories:** 48 (Weeks 1-48)
- **Format per story:** 20 lines (10 sentences + 10 discussion questions)
- **Sentence length:** 8-12 words average (TV-readable with Japanese translations displayed simultaneously)
- **ALL lines (1-20):** Emoji + English (8-12 words) + Japanese translation

**‚ö†Ô∏è CRITICAL DESIGN PRINCIPLE: NO PROGRESSION**

**All 48 stories are at the SAME Grade 6 difficulty level.** No progressive building to "mastery."

**Why No Progression:**
- Students can join the level at ANY week (Week 1, Week 25, Week 45, etc.)
- Students read stories in order starting from the week they join
- Week 1 must be the same level as Week 48
- Each story is self-contained

**Monthly Changes = VARIETY ONLY (NOT Progression):**
- Different themes for variety (school, hobbies, family, seasons, etc.)
- Same grammar pattern every week (Present Simple dominant)
- Vocabulary provides variety, not grammar complexity
- NO "mastery phase" or "integration phase"

**Every Single Story (Weeks 1-48) Must Have:**
- Present Simple: 8-10 sentences (dominant pattern)
- Be-verb (am/is/are): 4-6 sentences
- Present Continuous: 0-2 sentences MAX (current action ONLY)
- Basic Wh-questions: What, Where, Who ONLY
- Sentence length: 8-12 words average (TV-readable)
- Japanese translations: ALL 20 lines

This makes infinite content generation possible for beginners ‚Äî any week can be a student's first or last at Grade 6 level.

**Lines 1-10:** Story sentences (beginner content about a simple, relatable theme)

### Explanation: Why MIXED Pattern ALL YEAR?

**At Grade 6 beginner level:**

1. **Grammar is too simple for "focus weeks"**
   - Present Simple is everywhere, cannot isolate "focus" weeks
   - No natural progression like higher Eiken levels
   - Students need consistent reinforcement, not variety of grammar patterns

2. **Vocabulary is the primary challenge**
   - 300-500 words need recycling across 48 weeks
   - Students benefit from seeing core verbs (go, play, like, eat) repeatedly
   - Monthly themes provide vocabulary variety without grammar complexity

3. **Progression is flat by design**
   - Students can join ANY week (Stories 1-48 are same level)
   - No "I missed the Present Continuous week" problem
   - Each story is self-contained

4. **Exam reality (once exams exist) will show simple grammar**
   - Present Simple likely 80-90% of exam sentences
   - Present Continuous very limited (current action only)
   - No need for complex monthly rotation patterns

**Benefits of MIXED Pattern:**
- Students can start ANY week
- Vocabulary builds without forgetting basics
- Consistent reinforcement of Present Simple
- Better for real beginners who need repetition

**Forms:**

### Present Continuous Usage (VERY LIMITED)

### How to Maintain Clarity with Short Sentences

1. **Use simple subjects** (I, You, He, She, They, My friend, My family)
   - ‚úÖ Good: "My friend likes football." (5 words)

   - ‚úÖ Good: "I play tennis on Saturday." (6 words)

3. **Keep objects simple**
   - ‚úÖ Good: "I eat breakfast at seven o'clock." (7 words)
   - ‚ùå Too complex: "I eat a delicious breakfast of toast and eggs at seven o'clock." (12 words)

4. **One idea per sentence**

   - ‚ùå Better than: "It is spring today and the beautiful flowers are blooming everywhere." (12 words)

## 9. Content Quality Guidelines

### Primary Priority: Good, Readable Content

**Above all:** Stories should be engaging and well-written

- **Readability:** 8-12 word sentences ensure TV readability with translations
- **Simple tone:** Beginner-friendly, warm, encouraging
- **Topic relevance:** Elementary beginner topics (school, hobbies, family, daily routine)
- **Relatable content:** Stories should match students' actual experiences

### Secondary Priority: Humour and Engagement

**Use humour where appropriate to make content memorable:**

**Appropriate humour themes for Grade 6:**
- Silly school situations (dropping pencils, forgetting homework)
- Funny family moments (eating too much, funny pet tricks)
- Playful mishaps (losing a shoe, wearing clothes backwards)
- Simple wordplay appropriate for children

**Humour principles:**
- Gentle wit that children will understand
- Irony about real situations students recognize
- Playful commentary students relate to
- **AVOID:** Sarcasm, cynicism, mocking, or mean-spirited content

**When to use humour:**
- Opening lines can grab attention
- Examples can be playful
- Conclusions can warm the tone
- **When to avoid:** Serious topics or situations that might make students feel bad

### Tertiary Priority: Seasonal References (OPTIONAL, Not Required)

**Seasonal content is OPTIONAL ‚Äî do not force it**

**If using seasonal references:**
- Mention seasonal events only when they naturally relate to the topic
- Ensure seasonal events appear in their correct month:
  - Cherry blossoms: March-April (Japan)
  - Christmas: December
  - Halloween: October
  - Setsubun: February
  - Tanabata: July
  - Obon: August
- Don't force seasonal connections that feel artificial

**Better non-seasonal than weak seasonal:**
- ‚úÖ Excellent non-seasonal content about school day
- ‚ùå Weak seasonal reference about Christmas in July (forced and confusing)

### Topic Flexibility

**Stories can be about ANY elementary beginner topic:**
- School life (classroom, subjects, friends)
- Hobbies and sports (playing, activities)
- Family life (family members, home, helping)
- Nature and seasons (weather, outdoor play)
- Food and meals (breakfast, lunch, dinner)
- Daily routine (morning, school, after school, evening)

**Quality principle:** Theme flexibility allows creators to choose topics they can write well about
