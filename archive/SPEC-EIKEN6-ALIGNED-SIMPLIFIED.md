## 2. Grammar Priorities — Based on Announcement (SIMPLIFIED v2)

### Official Grammar Specification (From Announcement)

#### Present Simple (DOMINANT - Primary Focus)
- **Status: PRIMARY grammar feature** for Grade 6
- Uses: Daily routines, habits, facts, general truths
- Forms: I/You/We/They like/play/go. He/She/It likes/plays/goes.
- Questions: Do you...? Does he/she...?
- Negatives: I don't... He doesn't...

#### Present Continuous (EXTREMELY LIMITED - Use Sparingly)
- **Status: 0-1 sentences MAX per story**
- Uses: Actions happening RIGHT NOW ONLY
- Forms: I am playing. You are eating. He is running.
- Questions: Are you...? Is he...?
- ❌ **AVOID:** Present Continuous for future plans or general activities
- ✅ **ACCEPTABLE:** Look! He is running. (action NOW)

#### Basic Wh-Questions (RESTRICTED - Only 3 Wh-Words)
- **ALLOWED:** What, Where, Who ONLY
- What: Objects, preferences, identification
- Where: Locations, places
- Who: People identification
- ❌ **NOT ALLOWED:** Why, When, How (too complex for Grade 6)
- ❌ **NOT ALLOWED:** Which, Whose (too complex)

### Simplified Grammar Priorities (Educated Guess - v2)

#### Tier 1: Non-Negotiable (Present Simple EVERYWHERE)

| Priority | Feature | Why Critical | Usage |
|----------|---------|--------------|-------|
| #1 | **Present Simple (affirmative)** | DOMINANT | Every story |
| #2 | **Present Simple (negative)** | Essential | Every story |
| #3 | **Present Simple (questions)** | Required | Every story |
| #4 | **Be-verb (am/is/are)** | Foundation | Every story |

#### Tier 2: High Priority (LIMITED Use)

| Priority | Feature | Include When | Usage |
|----------|---------|--------------|-------|
| #5 | **Present Continuous** | Actions NOW | 0-1 sentences MAX |
| #6 | **Basic Wh-questions** | What/Where/Who only | 3-4 questions |
| #7 | **Be-verb questions** | Is/Are questions | 2-3 questions |

### Grammar to AVOID (Too Advanced for Grade 6 - Simplified)

| Grammar Feature | Why Avoid | Grade Level Where It Appears |
|----------------|-----------|----------------------------|
| Compound sentences with "and" | Too complex | Grade 4 and above |
| Present Continuous for general activities | Present Simple is correct | Grade 4 only for NOW |
| Modals beyond "can" | Too complex | Grade 5 and above |
| Past tense | Not in official spec | Grade 4 and above |
| Future (will/going to) | Not in official spec | Grade 4 and above |
| Comparative (+er/more) | Not in official spec | Grade 4 and above |
| Superlative (the most) | Not in official spec | Grade 4 and above |

## 4. Writing Guidelines — Simplified Complexity

### Sentence Length Strategy (Simplified)

**How to maintain simplicity with short sentences:**

1. **Use simple subjects** (I, You, My friend, My family)
   - *Good:* "My friend plays football." (5 words)
   - *Too complex:* "My friend who sits next to me in class plays football." (11 words)

2. **Use simple verbs** (like, play, go, eat, have)
   - *Good:* "I play tennis on Saturday." (6 words)
   - *Too complex:* "I usually enjoy practicing my favourite weekend sport." (10 words)

3. **Keep objects simple**
   - *Good:* "I eat breakfast at seven." (6 words)
   - *Too complex:* "I eat egg and toast for breakfast at seven o'clock." (11 words)

4. **One idea per sentence**
   - *Good:* "It is spring. Flowers bloom." (5 words)
   - *Wrong:* "It is spring and the beautiful flowers are blooming everywhere." (10 words)

### Story Structure Requirements

Each story must have exactly:
- **10 story sentences** (Present Simple dominant, 7-10 words each)
- **10 discussion questions** (personal questions, 7-10 words each)
- **Emojis** on all 20 lines
- **Japanese translations** on all 20 lines
- **Present Simple dominant** (8-10 sentences should be Present Simple)
- **MAX 1 sentence of Present Continuous** (only for current action NOW)

### Vocabulary Level Requirements (Simplified)

| Metric | Target (v2) | Notes |
|--------|-------------|-------|
| **Total vocabulary scope** | **~300 words** (was 300-500) | More realistic |
| **Average per story** | **12-15 new words** + recycled | Slower introduction |
| **Word family focus** | Basic everyday words | NO complex words |
| **Recycling priority** | **VERY HIGH reuse** | go, play, like, eat, have |

## 6. Common Mistakes to Avoid (Simplified)

1. **Sentences too long** → Keep sentences 7-10 words (TV readability)
2. **Too many new words** → 12-15 new words per story MAX
3. **Present Continuous overuse** → 0-1 sentences MAX per story
4. **Complex vocabulary** → Keep words basic (~300 word range)
5. **Compound sentences** → Use simple sentences, avoid "and" connecting long ideas
6. **Forgetting emojis** → EVERY line needs an emoji
7. **Missing Japanese** → EVERY line needs Japanese translation
