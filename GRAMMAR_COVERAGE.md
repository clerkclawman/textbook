## Grammar Points NOT Yet Covered

### 🔴 High Priority (Fundamental Differences)

#### Possessive Forms
- **Possessive Adjectives**: my, your, his, her, its, our, their
  - Japanese: noun + noun (no particle)
  - English: requires possessive adjective
  - Example: My book, your pen, his car

- **Possessive Pronouns**: mine, yours, his, hers, its, ours, theirs
  - Japanese: uses "no" particle
  - English: standalone possessive forms
  - Example: This is mine, That book is yours

#### Prepositions
- **Basic Prepositions**: in, on, at, to, from, with, by, for
  - Japanese: postpositions (ni, de, kara, made, to)
  - English: prepositions before nouns
  - Example: in the box, on the table, at school

- **Preposition Pairs**: to/from, with/without, for/from
  - Directional and relational concepts

#### Tense System
- **Present Simple**: I eat, He eats
  - Japanese: different verb conjugation
  - English: -s for third person singular
  - Example: I like apples, He likes apples

- **Past Simple**: I ate, He ate
  - Japanese: different past tense formation
  - English: irregular verbs
  - Example: I went to school, She ate lunch

- **Future Forms**: will, be going to
  - Japanese: different future construction
  - English: modal verbs or be going to
  - Example: I will go, He is going to eat

#### Modal Verbs
- **Can/Can't**: ability and permission
  - Japanese: different construction
  - English: modal verb + base verb
  - Example: I can swim, She can't run

- **Must/Have to**: obligation
  - Japanese: different obligation forms
  - English: modal verbs
  - Example: I must study, You have to go

- **Should/Ought to**: advice
  - Japanese: different advice forms
  - Example: You should eat vegetables

#### Have/Has
- **Possession**: I have, He has
  - Japanese: motsu, iru, aru
  - English: have/has
  - Example: I have a car, She has a cat

- **Have to**: obligation
  - Example: I have to go home

#### There is/There are
- **Existence**: There is a cat, There are dogs
  - Japanese: aru/iru
  - English: there + be
  - Example: There is a book on the table

#### Question Formation
- **Yes/No Questions**: Do/Does/Did
  - Japanese: particle "ka"
  - English: auxiliary verbs
  - Example: Do you like apples? Does he eat?

- **Wh- Questions**: What, Where, When, Who, Why, How
  - Japanese: different word order
  - English: question word + auxiliary
  - Example: What are you eating? Where is he going?

#### Negation
- **Present Simple Negative**: don't/doesn't
  - Japanese: different negative forms
  - Example: I don't like apples, He doesn't eat

- **Past Simple Negative**: didn't
  - Example: I didn't go, She didn't eat

- **Present Continuous Negative**: am not/isn't/aren't
  - Example: I'm not eating, He isn't sleeping

#### Adjectives
- **Adjective Position**: before noun vs predicate
  - Japanese: same form for both
  - English: different positions
  - Example: a big cat vs The cat is big

- **Adjective Comparison**: comparatives and superlatives
  - Japanese: different comparison system
  - English: -er/more, -est/most

#### Quantifiers
- **Countable/Uncountable**: much/many, a few/a little
  - Japanese: different quantifier system
  - Example: much water, many apples

- **Some/Any**: positive vs negative/questions
  - Example: I have some apples, I don't have any

- **Every/Each**: distribution
  - Example: Every student, Each person

#### Determiners
- **This/That/These/Those**: demonstrative determiners
  - Already covered in Section 1, but could be expanded

- **A/An/The**: article usage rules
  - Already covered in Section 5, but could be expanded

#### Pronouns (Advanced)
- **Reflexive Pronouns**: myself, yourself, himself, herself, itself, ourselves, themselves
  - Japanese: different reflexive forms

- **Indefinite Pronouns**: someone, anyone, everyone, something, anything, everything

#### Verb Patterns

- **Infinitives**: to + verb

- **Make/Let + Object + Verb**: causative

#### Conjunctions
- **And/But/Or**: basic conjunctions
  - Already used in some contexts, but could be explicit drills

- **Because/So**: cause and effect
  - Japanese: different causal structures
  - Example: I'm tired because I ran, I ran so I'm tired

- **If/When**: conditional and temporal
  - Japanese: different conditional forms

#### Adverbs
- **Adverb Formation**: -ly adverbs
  - Japanese: different adverb formation

- **Adverb Position**: where adverbs go
  - Japanese: different adverb placement
  - Example: He runs quickly, I always eat breakfast

#### Passive Voice

## Notes for Development

### Translation Consistency
- Maintain consistent Japanese translation patterns
- Use ～ている for present continuous
- Use ～ます for present simple
- Use ～ました for past simple
- Use ～ません for negation
- Use ～が for existence (there is/are)
- Use ～の for possession

### Verb Selection
- Prefer transitive verbs for SVO structure
- Use common, concrete verbs
- Rotate through different verb types
- Include both regular and irregular verbs

### Noun Selection
- Use concrete, familiar objects
- Include animals, food, school items, people
- Rotate through singular and plural forms
- Use Japanese names with honorifics for people

### Adjective Selection
- Use simple, common adjectives
- Include size, quality, color, feeling
- Rotate through positive and negative
- Keep sentences minimal

### Emoji Usage
- Include relevant emoji at start of each sentence
- Use context-appropriate emojis
- Maintain consistency across sections

### Punctuation
- English sentences end with `.`
- Japanese sentences end with `。`
- No mixing punctuation types
