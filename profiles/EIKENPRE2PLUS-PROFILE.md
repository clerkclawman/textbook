# EIKEN Pre-2 Plus (準2級+) - Reference Profile

**Source:** 2025 EIKEN Test Papers (1st, 2nd, 3rd Sessions)  
**Last Verified:** 2026-03-23  
**Status:** ✅ **Active Reference** (Use for all Eiken Pre-2 Plus content creation)

---

## 1. CEFR Alignment & Role
- **Level:** **A2/B1 Bridge** (Not pure B1, but the transition point)
- **Role:** The **"Complex Narrative & Formal Opinion" Level**. Students handle **past sequences** (Past Perfect), **hypotheticals** (Type 2 Conditionals), **complex descriptions** (Relative Clauses), and **formal/academic topics** (society, economy, climate change).

## 2. Carried Over from Pre-2 (Review Only)
*These are assumed known. Do not focus on them as the primary learning goal.*
- **Grammar:** Past Continuous, Advanced Passive, Reported Speech (backshift), Complex Conjunctions (`while`, `although`, `since`), Modals of Advice (`should`, `must`).
- **Vocab:** Abstract topics (history, culture, environment, government, policy, research, opinion, debate).
- **Sentence Length:** 10-16 words.

## 3. NEW Grammar Focus (The Core)
*These are the **only** new structures introduced at this level.*

| Grammar Point | Function | Example from 2025 Papers |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Past Perfect** | **NEW:** Sequence of past events | "He **had done** it." <br> "His teacher **had seen** his favorite movie." <br> "He **had checked** into the wrong hotel." <br> "How she **had spent** her high school days." |
| **Type 2 Conditionals (Light)** | **NEW:** Unreal/hypothetical present/future | "It **would be** great **if you could** share..." <br> "**If you could** share any advice..." <br> "Percent of Canadians **would not drink** beanless coffee." |
| **Complex Relative Clauses** | **NEW:** `who`, `which`, `that` with multiple clauses | "Clothing and food to anybody **who needs** them." <br> "A company **that developed** a straw..." <br> "The water **that comes out** is..." <br> "One of them, **which the company uses**, is..." |
| **Formal/Abstract Vocabulary** | **NEW:** Academic, societal, environmental topics | `society`, `economy`, `policy`, `climate change`, `sustainability`, `ethics`, `innovation`, `diversity`, `equity`, `leadership`, `management`, `research`, `study`, `survey`, `opinion`, `belief`, `argument`, `debate`. |
| **Advanced Modals** | **NEW:** `would`, `could`, `might` for hypotheticals | "It **would be** great if..." <br> "They **could make** a difference." <br> "Experts **believe** that..." |

### ❌ **Strictly Excluded (Do Not Use)**
- **Past Perfect Continuous** (had been + -ing) - *Rare to non-existent.*
- **Type 3 Conditionals** (If I had known...) - *Not tested.*
- **Subjunctive Mood** (I suggest that he be...) - *Not tested.*
- **Complex Passive (Perfect Continuous)** (has been being done) - *Avoid.*
- **Very Long Sentences** (>20 words) - *Keep under 18.*

## 4. Vocabulary Characterization
*Focus on **formal**, **academic**, **societal**, and **environmental** topics.*
- **Abstract Topics:** `society`, `culture`, `economy`, `policy`, `government`, `research`, `study`, `survey`, `opinion`, `belief`, `argument`, `debate`, `solution`, `problem`, `issue`, `challenge`, `change`, `development`, `progress`, `future`, `past`, `present`, `traditional`, `modern`, `global`, `local`, `national`, `international`, `environment`, `technology`, `education`, `health`, `community`, `history`, `philosophy`, `ethics`, `morality`, `justice`, `freedom`, `equality`, `rights`, `responsibility`, `leadership`, `management`, `innovation`, `creativity`, `artificial intelligence`, `climate change`, `sustainability`, `diversity`, `inclusion`, `equity`.
- **Time Markers:** `before`, `after`, `by the time`, `until`, `when`, `while`, `during`, `meanwhile`, `previously`, `formerly`, `currently`, `recently`, `lately`, `already`, `yet`, `just`, `ever`, `never`, `before`, `since`, `for`.
- **Opinion & Argument (Formal):** `argue`, `claim`, `state`, `mention`, `explain`, `describe`, `discuss`, `analyze`, `evaluate`, `compare`, `contrast`, `propose`, `recommend`, `suggest`, `advise`, `believe`, `think`, `feel`, `agree`, `disagree`, `support`, `oppose`, `favor`, `prefer`.
- **Verbs (Past Perfect):** `had + past participle` (done, seen, checked, spent, said, told, asked, answered, opened, closed, started, stopped, finished, bought, read, wrote, ran, walked, swam, flew, drove, slept, drank, eaten, taken, got, given, put, met, kept, built, bought, taught, caught, brought, heard, understood, spent, sent).
- **Verbs (Type 2):** `would`, `could`, `might` + base verb.

## 5. Sentence Structure & Length
- **Length:** **12-18 words** average. (Some up to 20 for complex ideas).
- **Structure:** Complex sentences with `who`, `which`, `that`, `before`, `after`, `by the time`, `until`, `while`, `although`, `because`, `if`, `when`, `since`, `as`, `and`, `but`, `so`.
  - *Example:* "He **had done** it **before** she **arrived**." (7 words)
  - *Example:* "It **would be** great **if you could** share..." (8 words)
  - *Example:* "A company **that developed** a straw **to produce** clean water..." (10 words)
- **Complexity:** Can combine multiple clauses with relative pronouns (`who`, `which`, `that`) and time markers (`before`, `after`, `by the time`).

## 6. Example Sentences (Direct from 2025 Tests)
*These are the exact patterns to emulate.*

**Past Perfect (Sequence):**
> "He **had done** it."
> "His teacher **had seen** his favorite movie."
> "He **had checked** into the wrong hotel."
> "How she **had spent** her high school days."
> "They **had said** something important."
> "After he **had finished** the work, he left."

**Type 2 Conditionals (Hypothetical):**
> "It **would be** great **if you could** share..."
> "**If you could** share any advice..."
> "Percent of Canadians **would not drink** beanless coffee."
> "**If** beanless coffee **were** available, many **would try** it."
> "I **would help** if I **had** time."

**Complex Relative Clauses:**
> "Clothing and food to anybody **who needs** them."
> "A company **that developed** a straw..."
> "The water **that comes out** is clean."
> "One of them, **which the company uses**, is..."
> "The book **that I read** was interesting."
> "The person **who called** yesterday is my friend."

**Formal/Abstract Topics:**
> "The **economy** of the city has changed."
> "**Climate change** is a major challenge."
> "The **policy** affects many people."
> "**Research** shows that... "
> "The **government** announced a new **plan**."
> "Experts **believe** that **sustainability** is key."

**Advanced Modals:**
> "It **would be** great if..."
> "They **could make** a difference."
> "Experts **might suggest** a different approach."
> "We **should consider** the **environmental impact**."
> "The **company** **must** follow **regulations**."

**Questions (Wh- & Yes/No):**
> "What is true about the **economy**?"
> "How does **climate change** affect **frogs**?"
> "Why do some people **oppose** the **policy**?"
> "Which **solution** is most **effective**?"
> "What **would** you **do** in this **situation**?"

## 7. Allowed Patterns Only (Strict Positive Constraints)
*The AI must ONLY use these patterns. If a pattern is not listed below, it is FORBIDDEN.*

### ✅ **ALLOWED Verb Forms**
- **Present Simple:** All base verbs.
- **Past Simple:** All common irregulars.
- **Past Continuous:** `was/were + [verb]-ing`.
- **Past Perfect:** `had + past participle`.
- **Present Perfect:** `have/has + past participle`.
- **Future:** `will`, `going to`, `can`, `could`, `may`, `might`, `would`, `should`, `must`, `have to`, `need to`.
- **Conditional (Type 1 & 2):** `If + Present, will/can` AND `If + Past, would/could/might`.
- **Comparatives/Superlatives:** `more`, `less`, `better`, `worse`, `best`, `worst`, `biggest`, `smallest`, `most`, `least`, `-er`, `-est`.
- **Passive (All Tenses):** `am/is/are/was/were/has been/have been/will be/had been + past participle`.
- **Reported Speech (Backshift):** `said that`, `told me`, `asked if`, `explained that`, `mentioned that`, `wondered`, `wanted to know`.
- **Relative Clauses:** `who`, `which`, `that`, `whose`, `whom`.

### ✅ **ALLOWED Question Words**
- `What`, `Where`, `Who`, `When`, `Why`, `How`, `How long`, `How many`, `How often`, `Which`, `Did`, `Was`, `Were`, `Will`, `Can`, `Could`, `Have`, `Has`, `Do`, `Does`, `Should`, `Must`, `Have to`, `Need to`, `Would`, `Might`.

### ✅ **ALLOWED Sentence Structures**
- **Simple SVO:** "I have been to Japan."
- **Complex (Relative Clauses):** "The book that I read was interesting."
- **Complex (Time Markers):** "After he had finished, he left."
- **Conditional (Type 1 & 2):** "If you have time, come." / "If I had time, I would come."
- **Passive:** "The road was closed." / "The ways have been limited." / "It had been done."
- **Reported:** "She said that he wanted to be an actor."
- **Compound:** "I went to the park, and I played soccer."

### ❌ **FORBIDDEN (If seen, the output is INVALID)**
- **Past Perfect Continuous:** `had been + -ing`.
- **Type 3 Conditionals:** `If I had known...`, `I would have gone...`.
- **Subjunctive Mood:** `If I were`, `I suggest that he be`.
- **Complex Passive (Perfect Continuous):** `has been being done`.
- **Very Long Sentences:** >20 words.
- **Informal/Colloquial Language:** Slang, contractions (unless natural), overly casual phrases.

## 8. AI Validation Checklist (Run Before Output)
*If ANY item is unchecked, the content is INVALID.*

- [ ] **Verb Check:** Does every verb match the "ALLOWED Verb Forms" list (Present, Past, Past Continuous, Past Perfect, Present Perfect, Future, Passive, Reported, Relative)?
- [ ] **Tense Check:** Is there **NO** Past Perfect Continuous (`had been + ing`)?
- [ ] **Conditional Check:** Are conditionals **Type 1 or 2** only (no Type 3)?
- [ ] **Question Check:** Are all questions using `What`, `Where`, `Who`, `When`, `Why`, `How`, `Have`, `Has`, `Did`, `Will`, `Can`, `Should`, `Must`, `Would`, `Could`?
- [ ] **Length Check:** Is every sentence **≤ 18 words** (average 14-15)?
- [ ] **Complexity Check:** Are compound/complex sentences using `who`, `which`, `that`, `before`, `after`, `by the time`, `until`, `while`, `although`, `because`, `if`, `when`, `since`, `as`, `and`, `but`, `so`?
- [ ] **Topic Check:** Is the topic formal/academic (society, economy, climate change, policy, research, ethics)?
