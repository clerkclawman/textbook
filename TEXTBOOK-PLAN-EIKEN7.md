## 5. Grammar Requirements per Story (CRITICAL)

### Be-Verb Sentences: 3-5 Instances
- **Target:** 3-5 instances per story (Lines 1-10 only)  
- **Forms:** is, are, am  
- **Uses:** Simple descriptions ("This is a cat."), locations ("My school is here."), qualities ("The sky is blue.")

### Present Simple Sentences: 5-7 Instances
- **Target:** 5-7 instances per story (Lines 1-10 only)  
- **Pattern:** Subject + Verb (s form for third person singular)  
- **Uses:** Actions ("I like dogs.", "She plays games."), habits ("We walk to school.")

### Yes/No Questions: 10 Total (Lines 11-20)
- **Target:** 10 questions total (Lines 11-20)  
- **Types:** 
  - Be-verb questions: "Is it red?", "Are they happy?" (4-5 questions)
  - Present Simple DO questions: "Do you like apples?", "Does he play?" (4-5 questions)
- **Answers:** One-syllable answers preferred ("Yes", "No", "Cat", "Red")

### Sentence Length: 5-8 Words (TV-Readable Constraint)
- **Average:** 5-8 words per sentence  
- **Maximum:** 10 words per sentence (hard limit)  
- **Apply to:** ALL 20 lines (both story sentences AND discussion questions)

### Grammar Simplicity Strategy

**CRITICAL: Use ONLY simple sentences**

**‚ùå WRONG (too complex):**
- *Compound:* "The cat is black and it likes to play." (9 words, conjunction "and")
- *Continuous:* "The cat is jumping on the bed." (7 words, Present Continuous)
- *Past tense:* "The cat jumped on the bed." (6 words, Past Simple)
- *Modal:* "The cat can jump on the bed." (7 words, modal "can")

**‚úÖ CORRECT (Grade 7 level):**
- *Simple Be-verb:* "The cat is black." (5 words)
- *Simple Present:* "The cat plays." (4 words)
- *Two simple sentences:* "The cat is black. It plays." (5 + 3 words, two simple sentences)

**Splitting Strategy:**
If content needs conjunctions or complex structures, split into two simple sentences:
- ‚ùå "I like apples and bananas and grapes." (7 words, multiple "and")
- ‚úÖ "I like apples. I like bananas. I like grapes." (4 + 4 + 4 words)

## 6. Content Quality Guidelines

### Primary Priority: Simple, Relatable Content
- **Above all:** Stories should be relatable to young children (ages 5-8 estimated)  
- **Readability:** 5-8 word sentences ensure TV readability with translations  
- **Simple tone:** Friendly, cheerful, easy to understand  
- **Topic relevance:** Everyday topics (family, animals, colours, food, toys, school, games, friends, seasons, routines, celebrations)

### Secondary Priority: Repetition and Pattern Recognition
**Use repetition to help young learners recognise patterns:**

**Repetition strategies:**
- Repeat key nouns ("The cat... The cat... The cat...")
- Repeat sentence structures ("It is red. It is blue. It is green.")
- Use familiar themes each week within a month
- Maintain consistent question patterns ("Is it...?", "Do you...?")

**Pattern recognition benefits:**
- Students recognise grammar THROUGH exposure (not through explicit teaching)
- Repetition builds confidence with each story
- Familiar patterns reduce cognitive load for very young learners

### Tertiary Priority: Positive Emotions and Joy
**Keep content positive and cheerful:**

**Positive emotion themes:**
- Happy families
- Cute animals
- Bright colours
- Tasty food
- Fun games
- Good friends
- Enjoyable routines
- Happy celebrations

**Emotion principles:**
- Focus on positive feelings (happy, love, fun, nice, good)
- Avoid negative emotions unless simple comparisons (happy vs. sad)
- Use cheerful emojis matching the content
- Keep stories uplifting and encouraging

### Topic Flexibility
**Stories should stay within the monthly theme but can vary:**

**Example flexibility within "Animals I Like" (February):**
- Story 5: Pets at home (cat and dog)
- Story 6: Farm visit (see farm animals)
- Story 7: Zoo trip (see big animals)
- Story 8: Garden bugs (find small animals)

**Quality principle:** Theme flexibility allows creators to choose child-friendly variations within each monthly topic

### Be-Verb Examples

**‚ùå INCORRECT (wrong grammar for Grade 7):**

### Present Simple Examples

**‚úÖ CORRECT (Grade 7 level):**
- "I like dogs." (4 words)
- "She plays games." (4 words)
- "They run fast." (4 words)
- "We eat lunch." (4 words)

**‚ùå INCORRECT (wrong grammar for Grade 7):**
- "She is playing." (4 words) ‚Äî Present Continuous is EXCLUDED
- "She played games." (4 words) ‚Äî Past Simple is EXCLUDED
- "She can play." (4 words) ‚Äî Modal "can" is EXCLUDED
- "She likes to play and run." (7 words) ‚Äî Conjunction "and" is EXCLUDED

### Question Examples

**‚úÖ CORRECT (Grade 7 level):**
- "Is it a cat?" (5 words) ‚Äî Be-verb Yes/No question
- "Are you happy?" (4 words) ‚Äî Be-verb Yes/No question

- "Does she play?" (4 words) ‚Äî Present Simple DO question

**‚ùå INCORRECT (wrong grammar for Grade 7):**
- "What is it?" (4 words) ‚Äî Complex Wh-question "What" is EXCLUDED
- "Why do you like it?" (6 words) ‚Äî Complex Wh-question "Why" is EXCLUDED
- "Where is the cat?" (5 words) ‚Äî Complex Wh-question "Where" is EXCLUDED

### Sentence Splitting Examples

**‚ùå WRONG (uses conjunctions ‚Äî NOT Grade 7 level):**
- "The cat is black and it is big." (9 words, conjunction "and")
