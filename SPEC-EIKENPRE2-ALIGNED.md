## 5. Weekly Pattern â€” Modal Dominance with Alternating Focus

### Core Pattern (Modals Focus Weeks)

**The 8-week rotating pattern:**

| Week | Type | Grammar Focus | Modals Emphasised | Notes |
|------|------|---------------|-------------------|-------|
| **Week 1** | MIXED | All modal types (light touch) | All 6 modals | Gentle introduction |
| **Week 2** | MODAL FOCUS | **Can/Could** (ability, possibility) | can, could, can't, couldn't | Primary focus week |
| **Week 3** | MIXED | All modal types (mixed practice) | All 6 modals | Reinforcement |
| **Week 4** | MODAL FOCUS | **Should/Must** (obligation, advice) | should, must, shouldn't, mustn't | Primary focus week |
| **Week 5** | MIXED | All modal types + 1st conditionals | All 6 modals + if...will | Conditionals introduced |
| **Week 6** | MODAL FOCUS | **Would/Might** (past future, possibility) | would, might, wouldn't | Primary focus week |
| **Week 7** | MIXED | All modal types + gerunds/infinitives | All 6 modals + -ing/to | verb patterns expand |
| **Week 8** | CONDITIONAL FOCUS | **1st Conditionals** (if...will) | if...will, unless, when | Conditionals mastery |

**Pattern rotates:** After Week 8, return to Week 1 and repeat with new themes/vocabulary.

### Why This Pattern Works

**Addressing the "Modal Explosion" reality:**
- Each week contains modals (minimum 8-12 modal uses per story)
- Focus weeks (2, 4, 6) intensify specific modal pairs
- Mixed weeks (1, 3, 5, 7) provide variety and cross-modal practice

**Building progressively:**
- **Weeks 1-2:** Can/Could foundation (simplest modal pair)
- **Weeks 3-4:** Should/Must (obligation/necessity)
- **Weeks 5-6:** Would/Might (more abstract)
- **Weeks 7-8:** Conditionals mastery (new feature)

### Detailed Weekly Breakdown

#### Weeks 1 and 3: MIXED (All Modal Types)

**Grammar focus:**
- Light practice of all 6 modals: can, could, should, must, would, might
- Balanced distribution (2 of each modal type per story)
- 1st conditionals introduced in Week 5 onwards

**Week 2 Focus: Can/Could**
- **Primary:** 4-6 uses of can/could per story
- **Secondary:** Light use of other modals (1-2 each)
- **Function:** Ability, permission, possibility, polite requests

**Week 4 Focus: Should/Must**
- **Primary:** 4-6 uses of should/must per story
- **Secondary:** Light use of other modals (1-2 each)
- **Function:** Obligation, necessity, advice, prohibition

**Week 6 Focus: Would/Might**
- **Primary:** 4-6 uses of would/might per story
- **Secondary:** Light use of other modals (1-2 each)
- **Function:** Future in the past, possibility, preference

#### Weeks 5 and 7: MIXED + Additional Features

**Week 5: Mix + 1st Conditionals**
- All 6 modals (balanced)
- **Plus:** 1-2 first conditional sentences
- Conditional patterns: *If + present, will + verb*

**Week 7: Mix + Gerunds/Infinitives**
- All 6 modals (balanced)
- **Plus:** 2-3 gerund/infinitive verb patterns

**Primary focus:** 1st Conditionals
- **3-4 conditional sentences per story**
- Patterns:

  - *Unless you study, you will fail.* (negative)

**Secondary:** Modals supporting conditionals
- *If you **can**, you **should** go.* (modal + conditional)

### Cultural Awareness Guidelines

#### 1. Referencing Multiple Regions without Confusion

**Strategic approaches:**
- **Primary context:** Japan (students' direct experience) serves as anchor
- **Secondary comparison:** Brief, explicit references to other regions are optional
- Comparisons should be natural, not forced

**Example pattern (optional, not required):**
> "In Japan, we enjoy cherry blossom viewing in April. In England, people celebrate Easter during spring. Both seasons bring beautiful weather and outdoor activities."

#### 2. Student-Friendly Topics for Pre-2 Level

**Why these topics work for Pre-2:**
- Pre-2 students are typically in high school or early university
- Topics should feel relevant to their life experiences
- Modal verbs naturally fit advice-giving and possibility discussions
- Topics can be about school, hobbies, future plans, everyday situations

**Ideal Pre-2 Topics:**
- **Education:** Exams, study methods, classroom experiences, teachers and classmates
- **Future plans:** Career goals, university choices, dreams and aspirations
- **Hobbies:** Sports, music, art, reading, video games
- **Travel:** Domestic travel, study abroad, cultural experiences
- **Technology:** Social media, smartphones, online learning
- **Social life:** Friends, family, celebrations, part-time work
- **Health and lifestyle:** Exercise, food, healthy habits, stress management

#### 3. Humour and Engagement (IDEAL to Include)

**Principles for Pre-2 humour:**
- Keep it light and relatable
- Focus on common student experiences
- Gentle irony about everyday situations
- Warm, inclusive humour (no mean-spirited content)

**Example humor themes:**
- Exam stress and procrastination ("I should start studying...tomorrow")
- Cafeteria food mysteries ("Is this really chicken?")
- Group projects where everyone waits until the last minute
- Forgetting homework and frantic excuses
- The awkwardness of speaking English in class
- Weekend plans that never quite happen
- Part-time job mishaps and awkward customers

**When to avoid humour:**
- Serious topics about social issues
- Personal or sensitive situations
- Anything that could be hurtful or divisive

### Sentence Length vs. Modal Density

**Strategy:** Keep sentences moderately long (15-18 words) while embedding modals naturally:

**Short sentences with modals:**
- *You can study at home.* (5 words â€” too simple)
- **Better:** *You can effectively study at home or in the library.* (9 words â€” still short)
- **Best (Pre-2 target):** *You can study more effectively at home or in the quiet library.* (13 words)

**Medium sentences with multiple modals:**
- *You should review your notes, and you must finish the assignment by Friday.* (14 words)
- **Better:** *You should review your notes carefully, and you must finish this assignment by Friday.* (15 words)

**Long sentences with modal + conditional:**
- *If you can learn these new words, you should be able to pass the exam.* (16 words)
- **Better:** *If you can learn these new vocabulary words, you should be able to pass the exam.* (17 words)
