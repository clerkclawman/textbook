## 1. CEFR Alignment & Role
- **Level:** **C1** (Advanced / Professional)
- **Role:** The **"Academic & Professional Mastery" Level**. Students demonstrate **sophisticated expression**, **nuanced argumentation**, **nominalization**, **subjunctive mood**, and **advanced inversion** in academic/professional contexts.

## 2. Carried Over from Pre-1 (Review Only)
*These are assumed known. Do not focus on them as the primary learning goal.*
- **Grammar:** Advanced Connectors (`Nevertheless`, `rarely`, `little`), Nominal Clauses (`the fact that`, `the reason why`), Complex Noun Phrases, Nuanced Critique.
- **Vocab:** Abstract/academic (perspectives, autonomy, management, culture, resolution, aggression, empowerment, rights, discrimination, systemic).
- **Sentence Length:** 12-22 words.

## 3. NEW Grammar Focus (The Core)
*These are the **only** new structures introduced at this level.*

| Grammar Point | Function | Example from 2025 Papers |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Subjunctive Mood (Limited)** | **NEW:** `Recommend that`, `suggest that`, `insist that` + bare infinitive | "Insist that Marty **get** physically fit." <br> "Suggest that he **come** tomorrow." <br> "Recommend that the policy **be** changed." |
| **Advanced Inversion** | **NEW:** `Had it not been`, `Not only... but also`, `Never...`, `Neither... nor` | "**Had it not been** for the Mongols, trade..." <br> "Dalí **had never intended** the painting to be seen..." <br> "**Neither** did A **nor** B..." |
| **Nominalization (Advanced)** | **NEW:** Converting verbs to nouns for sophistication | "Processes **incoming sensory data** and **reacts**..." <br> "**Interpretations reflect** what is..." <br> "**Developments** in..." |
| **Complex Nominal Clauses** | **NEW:** `The fact that`, `The idea that`, `The notion that` embedded in complex structures | "The **fact that** our brains interpret sensory data..." <br> "**What** differentiates his masterpiece..." <br> "**How** the brain **processes** incoming data..." |
| **Nuanced/Professional Vocabulary** | **NEW:** Deliberation, mechanism, resilience, adaptation, interpretation, neural networks | `deliberation`, `international`, `bigotry`, `thwart`, `mechanism`, `sensory`, `interpretation`, `neural networks`, `resilience`, `adaptation`, `symptoms`, `motivation`, `framework`, `outcomes`, `associations`, `anticipation`, `implication`, `consequence`, `causality`, `correlation`. |

### ❌ **Strictly Excluded (Do Not Use)**
- **C2-Level Nuance** (Advanced idioms, complex nominal clusters >3 words)
- **Very Long Sentences** (>25 words) - *Keep under 22.*
- **Archaic Language** (Unless quoting)
- **Overly Colloquial** (Unless in dialogue)
- **Jargon specific to one field** (Unless for specialized texts)

## 5. Sentence Structure & Length
- **Length:** **12-22 words** average. (Some up to 25 for complex ideas).
- **Structure:** Complex sentences with nominalization, advanced inversion (`Had it not been`), subjunctive mood, and sophisticated connectors (`However`, `Nevertheless`, `Not only... but also`).
  - *Example:* "**Had it not been** for the Mongols, trade **with the West** **never developed**." (13 words)
  - *Example:* "The brain **processes** incoming **sensory data** and **reacts** based on **calculations**." (12 words)
  - *Example:* "**Not only** did it **fail**, **but** it also **caused** harm to many." (12 words)
- **Complexity:** Can combine multiple clauses with nominalization, inversion, and advanced connectors.

## 7. Allowed Patterns Only (Strict Positive Constraints)
*The AI must ONLY use these patterns. If a pattern is not listed below, it is FORBIDDEN.*

### ✅ **ALLOWED Verb Forms**
- **Present Simple:** All base verbs.
- **Past Simple:** All common irregulars.
- **Past Continuous:** `was/were + [verb]-ing`.
- **Past Perfect:** `had + past participle`.
- **Present Perfect:** `have/has + past participle`.
- **Future:** `will`, `going to`, `can`, `could`, `may`, `might`, `would`, `should`, `must`, `have to`, `need to`.
- **Conditional (Type 1, 2, & 3):** `If + Present, will/can` AND `If + Past, would/could/might` AND `If + Past Perfect, would have...`.
- **Comparatives/Superlatives:** `more`, `less`, `better`, `worse`, `best`, `worst`, `biggest`, `smallest`, `most`, `least`, `-er`, `-est`.
- **Passive (All Tenses):** `am/is/are/was/were/has been/have been/will be/had been/could have been/would have been/might have been + past participle`.
- **Reported Speech (Complex):** `said that`, `told me`, `asked if`, `explained that`, `mentioned that`, `wondered`, `wanted to know`, `argued that`, `claimed that`, `suggested that`, `recommended that`, `questioned`, `doubted`, `skeptical`.
- **Relative Clauses (Advanced):** `who`, `which`, `that`, `whose`, `whom`, `where`, `when`, `at which`.
- **Nominal Clauses (Complex):** `the fact that`, `the reason why`, `the idea that`, `the notion that`, `what`, `whatever`, `whoever`, `whichever`, `whether`, `how`, `why`, `when`, `where`.
- **Subjunctive Mood:** `recommend that`, `suggest that`, `insist that`, `demand that`, `essential that` + bare infinitive.
- **Advanced Inversion:** `Had it not been`, `Not only... but also`, `Never...`, `Neither... nor`.

### ✅ **ALLOWED Question Words**
- `What`, `Where`, `Who`, `When`, `Why`, `How`, `How long`, `How many`, `How often`, `Which`, `Did`, `Was`, `Were`, `Will`, `Can`, `Could`, `Have`, `Has`, `Do`, `Does`, `Should`, `Must`, `Have to`, `Need to`, `Would`, `Could`, `Might`, `Whether`.

### ✅ **ALLOWED Sentence Structures**
- **Simple SVO:** "I have been to Japan."
- **Complex (Nominal Clauses):** "The fact that our brains interpret sensory data is remarkable."
- **Complex (Subjunctive):** "Recommend that the policy be changed."
- **Complex (Inversion):** "Had it not been for the Mongols, trade never developed."
- **Conditional (Type 1, 2, & 3):** "If you have time, come." / "If I had time, I would come." / "If I had known, I would have gone."
- **Passive (All Tenses):** "The road was closed." / "It has been limited." / "It had been done." / "It would have been done."
- **Reported (Complex):** "She said that he wanted to be an actor." / "He argued that the policy should change."
- **Compound:** "I went to the park, and I played soccer."

### ❌ **FORBIDDEN (If seen, the output is INVALID)**
- **C2-Level Nuance:** Advanced idioms, complex nominal clusters >3 words.
- **Very Long Sentences:** >25 words.
- **Archaic Language:** Unless quoting historical texts.
- **Overly Colloquial:** Informal language, slang (unless in dialogue).
- **Field-Specific Jargon:** Unless the context is specialized.

## 8. AI Validation Checklist (Run Before Output)
*If ANY item is unchecked, the content is INVALID.*

- [ ] **Verb Check:** Does every verb match the "ALLOWED Verb Forms" list (Present, Past, Past Continuous, Past Perfect, Present Perfect, Future, Passive (all tenses), Reported (complex), Relative (advanced), Nominal (complex), Subjunctive, Inversion)?
- [ ] **Tense Check:** Is there **NO** C2-Level Nuance** (advanced idioms, complex nominal clusters)?
- [ ] **Conditional Check:** Are conditionals **Type 1, 2, or 3 only** (no mixed or impossible conditionals unless for emphasis)?
- [ ] **Question Check:** Are all questions using `What`, `Where`, `Who`, `When`, `Why`, `How`, `Have`, `Has`, `Did`, `Will`, `Can`, `Should`, `Must`, `Would`, `Could`, `Might`, `Whether`?
- [ ] **Length Check:** Is every sentence **≤ 22 words** (average 13-14)?
- [ ] **Complexity Check:** Are compound/complex sentences using nominalization, advanced inversion (`Had it not been`), subjunctive mood, and sophisticated connectors (`However`, `Nevertheless`, `Not only... but also`)?
- [ ] **Topic Check:** Is the topic highly academic/professional (deliberation, international, bigotry, thwart, mechanism, sensory, interpretation, neural networks, resilience, adaptation, symptoms, motivation, framework)?
