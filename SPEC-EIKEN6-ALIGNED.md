## 3. Grammar Priorities â€” Based on Announcement (PROVISIONAL)

### Official Grammar Specification (From Announcement)

#### Present Simple (DOMINANT â€” Primary Focus)
- **Status: PRIMARY grammar feature** for Grade 6
- Uses: Daily routines, habits, facts, general truths, likes/dislikes
- Forms: I/You/We/They like/play/go. He/She/It likes/plays/goes.
- Questions: Do you...? Does he/she...?
- Negatives: I don't... He doesn't...

#### Present Continuous (VERY LIMITED â€” Use Sparingly)
- **Status: RESTRICTED to current actions ONLY**
- Uses: Actions happening RIGHT NOW (not general activities)
- Forms: I am playing. You are eating. He is running.
- Questions: Are you...? Is he...?
- âŒ **AVOID:** Present Continuous for future plans (unlike higher Eiken levels)
- âŒ **AVOID:** Present Continuous for temporary situations (use Present Simple)
- âœ… **ACCEPTABLE:** Look! He is running. (action NOW)

#### Basic Wh-Questions (RESTRICTED TYPE â€” Only 3 Wh-Words)
- **ALLOWED:** What, Where, Who ONLY
- What: Objects, preferences, identification ("What is this?", "What do you like?")
- Where: Locations, places ("Where is he?", "Where do you go to school?")
- Who: People identification ("Who is your teacher?", "Who is sitting there?")
- âŒ **NOT ALLOWED:** Why, When, How (too complex for Grade 6)
- âŒ **NOT ALLOWED:** Which, Whose (too complex for Grade 6)

#### Be-Verb Questions
- Is he...? Are you...? Am I...?
- Uses: States, descriptions, simple identification
- Examples: "Is he happy?", "Are you a student?"

### Cultural Awareness Guidelines for Grade 6

#### 1. Simple, Direct References without Confusion

**Grade 6-appropriate approaches:**
- **Primary context:** Students' direct daily experience (school, family, hobbies)
- **Simple language:** 1-2 sentences per seasonal reference maximum
- **Clear connections:** Seasonal events must directly relate to the story topic
- **Positive tone:** Celebrations and fun activities, not complex cultural analysis

**Example pattern (Grade 6 appropriate):**

#### 2. Beginner-Friendly Topics

**Why simple topics matter for Grade 6:**
- Grade 6 students have limited English vocabulary
- Complex cultural concepts are beyond their comprehension
- Seasonal events should be concrete and observable
- Focus on what students can SEE and DO, not abstract concepts

**Implementation guidelines:**
- **Concrete events:** What students can observe (flowers, snow, festivals)
- **Action-oriented:** What students do (play, eat, celebrate)
- **Simple vocabulary:** Words within 300-500 target range
- **Visual focus:** Seasons are visible (colourful flowers, white snow, green trees)

#### 3. Avoiding Cultural Stereotypes

**Principles:**
- **Specific examples over generalisations:** Reference specific events, not "Japanese culture"
- **Simple descriptions:** Basic facts about holidays, not detailed explanations
- **Child-friendly focus:** Activities children experience, not adult ceremonies
- **Multiple perspectives when relevant:** Some families celebrate differently

**What to avoid:**
- âŒ "Japanese people love cherry blossoms and always eat special food"

**What to avoid:**
- âŒ "All children in England wear uniforms to school"

### Sentence Length Strategy (Grade 6 Simplicity)

**How to maintain clarity with short sentences:**

1. **Use simple subjects** (I, You, He, She, They, My friend, My family)
   - *Good:* "My friend likes football." (5 words)
   - *Too complex:* "My best friend who lives near my house really enjoys playing." (12 words)

2. **Use simple verbs** (like, play, go, eat, have, is, are)
   - *Good:* "I play tennis on Saturday." (6 words)
   - *Too complex:* "I usually enjoy practicing my favourite sport during the weekend." (11 words)

3. **Keep objects simple**
   - *Good:* "I eat breakfast at seven o'clock." (7 words)
   - *Too complex:* "I eat a delicious breakfast of toast and eggs at seven o'clock." (12 words)

4. **One idea per sentence**
   - *Good:* "It is spring today. The flowers are beautiful." (8 words)
   - *Better than:* "It is spring today and the beautiful flowers are blooming everywhere." (12 words)
