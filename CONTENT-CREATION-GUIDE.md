## 📏 Sentence Length by Level (CEFR-Aligned)

| Level | CEFR | Target Words | Grammar Focus | Example |
|-------|------|-------------|---------------|---------|
| Eiken 7 | - (grade 3-4) | 3-4 words | Present simple only | "I like cats." |
| Eiken 6 | - (grade 5-6) | 4-5 words | Present simple + be verbs | "She is playing now." |
| Eiken 5 | A1 | 4-5 words | Present simple/continuous | "They play soccer every day." |
| Eiken 4 | A1 | 5-6 words | Past simple, will, comparatives | "He went to the park yesterday." |
| Eiken 3 | A1 | 6-7 words | Present perfect, longer A1 sentences | "I have never been to Osaka." |
| **Pre-2** | **A2** | **7-9 words** | **Modals, conditionals, passive** | "She decided to wash the dishes after dinner." |
| **Pre-2 Plus** | **B1** | **9-11 words** | **Perfect tenses, reported speech** | "Yuki had been working on her scholarship essays since last month." |
| Eiken 2 | A2/B1 | 10-12 words | Complex clauses, passives | "The report had been completed before the meeting started." |
| Pre-1 | B2 | 12-15 words | Academic discourse, argumentation | "Furthermore, the research demonstrates a significant correlation between..." |
| Eiken 1 | C1 | 15+ words | Sophisticated argumentation | "Moreover, the implications of this finding extend beyond the immediate scope..." |

### Key CEFR Boundaries
- **A1:** Eiken 5, 4, 3 (basic sentences, gradual length increase)
- **A2 starts at Pre-2** (modals, conditionals, passive voice)
- **Big jumps:** Eiken 3→Pre-2 (A1→A2), Pre-2→Pre-2+ (A2→B1)

**DO NOT exceed these limits.** Longer sentences = wrong level.

## ✅ Question Rules - CRITICAL!

### Rule 1: Questions Must NEVER Assume

**WRONG (assumes something not necessarily true):**
- "How is your husband?" (assumes they HAVE a husband)
- "How do you prepare for competitions?" (assumes they HAVE competitions)
- "What does your father do?" (assumes they HAVE a father)
- "How do you share information with neighbors?" (assumes they DO share)
- "Where do you go on vacation?" (assumes they GO on vacation)

**RIGHT (no assumptions - can answer "no" or "I don't"):**
- "Do you have a husband?" or "Are you married?"
- "Do you prepare for competitions?" or "Would you prepare for a competition?"
- "Do your parents work?" or "What do your parents do?" (safer)
- "Do you share information with neighbors?" or "Would you share...?"
- "Do you go on vacation?" or "Have you ever been on vacation?"

### Rule 2: Use "Ever" for Experience Questions

- ✅ "Have you **ever** been to a concert?"
- ✅ "Have you **ever** tried sushi?"
- ❌ "How often do you go to concerts?" (assumes they go)

### Rule 3: Use "Would" for Hypotheticals

- ✅ "**Would** you like to travel abroad?"
- ✅ "**Would** you help someone in need?"
- ❌ "How do you travel abroad?" (assumes they do)

### Rule 4: Yes/No or Open "Do/Have" Questions Are Safest

- ✅ "Do you like animals?"
- ✅ "Have you ever helped someone?"
- ✅ "Are you good at drawing?"
- ✅ "What makes you feel proud?" (everyone has felt proud of something)
- ✅ "How do you feel when...?" (universal emotions)

### Rule 5: Avoid These Question Starters

| Avoid | Use Instead |
|-------|-------------|
| "How do you..." (action) | "Do you..." or "Would you..." |
| "What does your..." (possession) | "Do you have..." |
| "Where do you..." (assumes action) | "Do you ever..." |
| "When do you..." (assumes frequency) | "Have you ever..." |

## 📝 Story Format Requirements

Each story must have:
1. **10 narrative lines** with:
   - Emoji at start
   - English sentence (correct word count for level)
   - Japanese translation
   - TPRS structure (problem → resolution)

2. **10 standalone questions** with:
   - Emoji at start
   - English question (no story references!)
   - Japanese translation
   - NO assumptions about student's life
   - Answerable by ANY student

## 🎯 Grammar Progression (All 10 Levels, CEFR-Aligned)

| Level | CEFR | Grammar Focus |
|-------|------|--------------|
| Eiken 7 | - | Present simple only (I like, She is) |
| Eiken 6 | - | Present simple + be verbs + some Present Continuous |
| Eiken 5 | A1 | Present simple, present continuous |
| Eiken 4 | A1 | Past simple, future with will, comparatives |
| Eiken 3 | A1 | Present perfect, superlatives, conditionals type 1 |
| **Pre-2** | **A2** | **Modals (can, should, must, would), passive voice, past continuous** |
| **Pre-2 Plus** | **B1** | **Past perfect, past perfect continuous, passive voice, reported speech, conditionals** |
| Eiken 2 | A2/B1 | Complex clauses, relative clauses, all passive forms |
| Pre-1 | B2 | Academic discourse markers, argumentation, formal register |
| Eiken 1 | C1 | Sophisticated argumentation, nuanced expression, advanced syntax |

### Key Reminders
- **A1 (Eiken 5, 4, 3):** Short sentences, basic grammar, gradual complexity increase
- **A2 starts at Pre-2:** This is where modals, conditionals, and passive voice begin
- **Pre-2 Plus is B1:** NOT A2 — uses advanced grammar like past perfect and reported speech

## 🔍 Quality Checklist

Before submitting any content, verify:

- [ ] Sentence length matches target level (count words!)
- [ ] Every line has emoji + English + Japanese
- [ ] Questions are standalone (don't require story knowledge)
- [ ] Questions make NO assumptions about student's life
- [ ] Questions use "ever," "would," or "do/are/have" forms
- [ ] TPRS narrative structure (setup → problem → resolution)
- [ ] Grammar matches level requirements
- [ ] Vocabulary is appropriate (not too easy, not too hard)

---

## 🚨 Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Assuming family structure**: Don't assume parents, siblings, husband, children
2. **Assuming activities**: Don't assume they play sports, travel, have hobbies
3. **Assuming possessions**: Don't assume they have pets, cars, phones
4. **Story-specific questions**: Questions must work without reading the story
5. **Wrong sentence length**: Count words! 10-11 for Pre-2 Plus, not 15+

## 📚 Example: Good vs Bad Questions

### Bad (makes assumptions):
- ❌ "How does your father help you?" (assumes father present + helpful)
- ❌ "What sports do you play?" (assumes they play sports)
- ❌ "How do you travel to school?" (assumes they go to school + have a method)
- ❌ "What does your best friend think?" (assumes they HAVE a best friend)

### Good (no assumptions):
- ✅ "Do your parents help you?" or "Does anyone help you?"
- ✅ "Do you play any sports?" or "Would you like to try a sport?"
- ✅ "Do you go to school?" or "How would you travel to school?"
- ✅ "Do you have a best friend?" or "Do you have friends?"
