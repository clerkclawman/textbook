## 2. Grammar Focus

### **Core Grammar Points**

#### **A. Present Simple (Dominant)**
- **Usage:** Habits, routines, facts, general truths
- **Structure:** Subject + base verb (add -s/-es for 3rd person singular)
- **Examples from 2025 tests:**
  - "I **play** soccer."
  - "She **likes** tennis."
  - "He **goes** to school."
  - "They **are** Canadian."
  - "I **have** two brothers."
  - "We **watch** basketball games."

#### **B. Present Continuous (Limited)**
- **Usage:** Actions happening now
- **Structure:** Subject + am/is/are + verb-ing
- **Examples:**
  - "What **is** Mike **doing** now?"
  - "He **is swimming**."
  - "What **is** Scott **doing** now?"
  - "He **is** [reading/watching]."

#### **C. Be-Verb (is/am/are)**
- **Usage:** Identity, location, description
- **Examples:**
  - "Where **is** Ken?"
  - "He **'s** in the garden."
  - "This **is** my room."
  - "I **'m** Tom."
  - "It **is** very cold."
  - "Your T-shirt **is** nice."

#### **D. Can/Can't (Ability/Permission)**
- **Usage:** Ability, requests, permission
- **Examples:**
  - "**Can** you help me?"
  - "**Can** I [do something]?"
  - "I **can't** play soccer today."
  - "**Can** you come to my house?"

#### **E. Do/Does Questions**
- **Usage:** Asking about habits, preferences, facts
- **Examples:**
  - "**Do** you play basketball?"
  - "**Do** you have any sisters?"
  - "**What do** you do on weekends?"
  - "I **do**, too."

#### **F. Basic Wh- Questions**
- **What:** "What's your favorite color?" / "What's the date today?"
- **Where:** "Where is Ken?" / "Where are my pajamas?"
- **When:** "When do you study English?"
- **Who:** (Limited use, mostly in listening)

#### **G. Let's (Suggestions)**
- **Usage:** Making suggestions
- **Examples:**
  - "**Let's** play badminton."
  - "**Let's** start today's lesson."

### **Grammar NOT Present (or Extremely Rare)**
- ❌ Past tense (was/were, -ed verbs)
- ❌ Future tense (will, going to)
- ❌ Present perfect (have/has + past participle)
- ❌ Comparatives/superlatives (bigger, best)
- ❌ Conditionals (if...)
- ❌ Passive voice
- ❌ Reported speech

## 3. Vocabulary Profile

### **Core Vocabulary Categories**

#### **A. Personal Information**
- Names: Tom, Jane, Ken, Amy, Bob, Brad, Sam, Taro, Julia, Peter, Scott, Keiko, Megumi, Fred, Allan, Steve
- Family: mother, father, sister, brother
- Pronouns: I, you, he, she, it, we, they, my, your, his, her

#### **B. School & Learning**
- Subjects: English, music, art
- People: teacher, student
- Objects: computer, camera, book, word
- Actions: study, learn, help, start, lesson

#### **C. Daily Life & Routines**
- Time: today, now, tomorrow, weekend, Sunday, Monday
- Activities: play, go, watch, like, have, do, come, start
- Places: school, park, house, garden, room

#### **D. Sports & Hobbies**
- Sports: soccer, basketball, tennis, badminton, volleyball
- Verbs: play, watch, swim, run
- Equipment: ball, camera, violin

#### **E. Weather & Seasons**
- Weather: cold, hot, cloudy, sunny
- Seasons: spring, summer, fall, winter (limited)

#### **F. Colors & Descriptions**
- Colors: red, blue, green, yellow, brown, pink
- Adjectives: nice, cute, short, long, big, small, new, old

#### **G. Food & Drink**
- Limited: milk, water, apple (basic items)

#### **H. Body & Health**
- Body parts: (very limited)
- Health: cold, sick (basic)

### **Vocabulary Characteristics**
- **Word Length:** Mostly 1-2 syllables
- **Frequency:** High-frequency words used in daily conversation
- **Context:** Concrete, tangible concepts (not abstract)
- **Total Unique Words:** ~300-400 in core test vocabulary

## 5. Question Types in Test

### **A. Listening (Dialogue Completion)**
- Short conversations (2-3 lines)
- Fill in the missing response
- Focus on appropriate social responses

### **B. Reading (Multiple Choice)**
- Single sentence with blank
- Choose correct verb form (is/are, do/does, can/can't)
- Choose correct word (color, object, action)

### **C. Sentence Ordering (Limited)**
- Arrange 2-3 words into a sentence
- Simple subject-verb-object order

## 6. CEFR Alignment Justification

### **Why Below A1?**
1. **No past tense:** Cannot talk about past events
2. **No future tense:** Cannot talk about plans or predictions
3. **No complex sentences:** All sentences are simple (one clause)
4. **Limited vocabulary:** ~300-600 words vs. A1's ~500-800
5. **No abstract concepts:** Only concrete, immediate topics
6. **No conditionals:** Cannot express hypothetical situations

### **A1 Threshold (Not Met)**
- A1 requires: ability to talk about past (simple past), basic future (will/going to), and slightly more complex questions
- Eiken 5 stays firmly in **present simple** and **basic questions only**

### **Pattern 4: Ability/Permission**
- "Can you [verb]?"
- "Can I [verb]?"
- "I can/can't [verb]."

### **Pattern 5: Possession**
- "Do you have [object]?"
- "I have [number] [object]."
- "This is my [object]."

## 8. Content Creation Guidelines (For Reference)

### **DO:**
- ✅ Use present simple tense exclusively
- ✅ Keep sentences 3-5 words max
- ✅ Focus on concrete, daily life topics
- ✅ Use high-frequency vocabulary
- ✅ Create simple Wh- questions (What/Where/When/Who)
- ✅ Include Yes/No questions with do/does/can

### **DON'T:**
- ❌ Use past tense verbs (went, saw, had)
- ❌ Use future forms (will, going to)
- ❌ Use present perfect (have done, has been)
- ❌ Use comparatives/superlatives (bigger, best)
- ❌ Use conditionals (if, when + future)
- ❌ Use sentences longer than 5-6 words
- ❌ Use abstract or complex topics
