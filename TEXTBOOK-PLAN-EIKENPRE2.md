## 1. Overview

### Curriculum Summary
- **Total stories:** 48 (Weeks 1-48)
- **Format per story:** 20 lines (10 sentences + 10 discussion questions)
- **Sentence length:** 15-18 words average (TV-readable with Japanese translations displayed simultaneously)
- **ALL lines (1-20):** Emoji + English (15-18 words) + Japanese translation

**‚ö†Ô∏è CRITICAL DESIGN PRINCIPLE: NO PROGRESSION**

**All 48 stories are at the SAME Ê∫ñ2Á¥ö difficulty level.** No progressive building to "mastery."

**Why No Progression:**
- Students can join the level at ANY week (Week 1, Week 25, Week 45, etc.)
- Students read stories in order starting from the week they join
- Week 1 must be the same level as Week 48
- Each story is self-contained

**Weekly Changes = Variety Only (NOT Progression):**
- 8-week rotating pattern provides variety in modal focus
- Different topics for variety (school, hobbies, travel, technology, etc.)
- **MODAL EXPLOSION:** Every story contains 8-12 modal verbs (minimum 8)
- 1st conditionals introduced from Week 5 onwards (1-3 instances)
- Gerunds/infinitives from Week 7 onwards (2-3 instances)

**Every Single Story (Weeks 1-48) Must Have:**
- Modal verbs: 8-12 instances (STRICT MINIMUM = 8)
- Modal variety: At least 4 different modal types per story
- 1st conditionals: 1-3 instances (Week 5+ only)
- Gerunds/infinitives: 2-3 instances (Week 7+ only)
- Sentence length: 15-18 words (max 22)
- Japanese translations: ALL 20 lines

This makes infinite content generation possible for a real skill ‚Äî any week can be a student's first or last at Ê∫ñ2Á¥ö level.

**Modal Usage Examples:**
- *Can:* "You can learn new skills with practice." (ability)
- *Could:* "You could join a study group." (possibility/suggestion)

- *Must:* "You must finish your homework." (obligation)
- *Would:* "I would like to travel." (preference)
- *Might:* "It might rain tomorrow." (possibility)

### 1st Conditionals: 1-3 Instances (Week 5+ Only)

| Requirement | Target |
|-------------|--------|
| **1st conditional sentences** | 1-3 instances (Week 5+ only) |
| **If...will patterns** | Standard conditional form |
| **Unless + present** | Negative condition |
| **When + present** | Certainty (when, not if) |

**Examples:**
- *If you study, you will pass.*  
- *You won't succeed unless you practice.*  
- *When I finish, I will call you.*

### Gerunds/Infinitives: 2-3 Instances (Week 7+ Only)

**Examples:**
- *I enjoy reading books.* (gerund)

## 7. Content Quality Guidelines

### Primary Priority: Good, Readable Content
- **Above all:** Stories should be engaging and well-written  
- **Readability:** 15-18 word sentences ensure TV readability with translations  
- **Student-friendly tone:** Conversational, relatable, accessible to high school/university students  
- **Topic relevance:** School life, hobbies, travel, technology, future plans, everyday situations

### Secondary Priority: Humour and Engagement
**Use humour where appropriate to make content memorable:**

**Appropriate humour themes for Ê∫ñ2Á¥ö:**
- Exam stress and last-minute studying
- Procrastination and "I'll start tomorrow"
- Cafeteria food mysteries ("Is this really chicken?")
- Group projects where everyone waits until the deadline
- Forgetting homework and creating frantic excuses
- Speaking English nervously in class
- Part-time job awkward moments and strange customers
- Weekend plans that constantly change

**Humour principles:**
- Gentle wit that students will recognise
- Irony about everyday student situations
- Playful commentary students relate to
- Warm, inclusive humour (no mean-spirited content)
- **Avoid:** Sarcasm, cynicism, mocking, or hurtful content

### Tertiary Priority: Seasonal References (OPTIONAL, Not Required)
**Seasonal content is OPTIONAL ‚Äî do not force it**

**If using seasonal references:**
- Mention seasonal events only when they naturally relate to the topic
- Ensure seasonal events appear in their correct month:
  - New Year: January
  - Cherry blossoms: March-April
  - Golden Week: April-May
  - Golden Week: April-May
  - Halloween: October
- Don't force seasonal connections that feel artificial

**Better non-seasonal than weak seasonal:**
- ‚úÖ Excellent non-seasonal content about study methods  
- ‚ùå Weak seasonal reference about Christmas in July (forced and confusing)

### Topic Flexibility
**Stories can be about ANY student-appropriate topic:**
- School and study (exams, homework, classroom, clubs)
- Hobbies and interests (sports, music, reading, games)
- Travel and experiences (trips, festivals, cultural events)
- Future and goals (career, university, dreams)
- Daily life and relationships (friends, family, routines)
- Technology and modern life (smartphones, social media)

**Quality principle:** Theme flexibility allows creators to choose topics they can write well about

## Appendix C: 1st Conditional Patterns

### Conditional Sentence Structures

| Pattern | Structure | Example | Notes |
|---------|-----------|---------|-------|
| **Standard conditional** | If + present simple, will + verb | *If you study, you will pass.* | Most common pattern |
| **Negative condition** | Unless + present simple | *You won't pass unless you study.* | "Unless" = "if not" |
| **Certainty** | When + present simple | *When I finish, I will call.* | Use "when" for certainty |
| **Modal + conditional** | If + present, modal + verb | *If you can, you should go.* | Combines modals + conditions |

**Target:** 1-3 conditional sentences per story (Week 5+ only).

## Appendix D: Gerund/Infinitive Patterns

### Verb Pattern Structures

| Pattern | Verbs | Example | Notes |
|---------|-------|---------|-------|
| **Verb + gerund** | enjoy, avoid, practice, finish | *I enjoy reading books.* | Use gerund after these verbs |
| **Verb + infinitive** | want, need, decide, hope | *I want to learn English.* | Use infinitive after these verbs |
| **Verb + both** | start, begin, continue | *I start studying.* OR *I start to study.* | Both are correct |

**Target:** 2-3 verb pattern instances per story (Week 7+ only).
