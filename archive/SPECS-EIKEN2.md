# Eiken 2 (2級) - Content Specifications

## Level Overview

- **Target:** High School Graduate (高校卒業程度)
- **CEFR:** B1 (intermediate)
- **Vocabulary:** 5,000-7,500 words
- **Story Count:** 48
- **Primary Focus:** Complex sentences, formal register, academic vocabulary
- **Critical Transition:** Bridge between junior high and university level

---

## Grammar Specifications

### Required Grammar Points

#### All Tenses and Aspects
- Present simple, continuous, perfect, perfect continuous
- Past simple, continuous, perfect, perfect continuous
- Future (will, going to, present continuous)
- Full range of time expressions

#### Relative Clauses
- **Defining:** The man who lives next door is a teacher
- **Non-defining:** My brother, who is 18 years old, is a university student
- ** whom, which, where, when, why**
- Sentences can now have relative clauses at various positions

#### Conditionals (Types 0, 1, 2, 3)
- **Type 0 (General truths):** If you heat water, it boils
- **Type 1 (Future):** If it rains tomorrow, I will stay home
- **Type 2 (Present untrue):** If I were rich, I would travel the world
- **Type 3 (Past untrue):** If I had studied harder, I would have passed the exam

#### Complex Modals
- **Modals in past:** must have done, could have gone, should have seen
- **Necessity and obligation:** must, have to, should, ought to
- **Advice and suggestion:** should, ought to, had better
- **Speculation:** might, could, may have, might have

#### Passive Voice (Advanced)
- All tenses in passive (present, past, future, perfect)
- Agent vs. agentless passives
- Passive with modal verbs (can be done, should be made, must be completed)

#### Complex Sentences
- Compound-complex structures
- Multiple subordinate clauses
- Reported speech with various reporting verbs (he said that, she explained why, they argued about)

## Vocabulary Characteristics

### Word Types
- **Academic vocabulary:** university, major, degree, professor, psychology, economics
- **Business words:** presentation, meeting, project, deadline, collaboration
- **Social issue terms:** discrimination, education, healthcare, environment, technology
- **Professional contexts:** interview, resume, employment, career advancement
- **Abstract concepts:** success, failure, opportunity, challenge, achievement

### Vocabulary Complexity
- **2-4 syllables:** More complex vocabulary allowed
- **Low-frequency but important:** Some less common but relevant words
- **Academic register:** Words used in academic/professional contexts
- **Sophisticated expressions:** More nuanced and subtle meanings

### Common Topics
- University life (majors, exams, campus life, study abroad)
- Part-time jobs (interviews, responsibilities, work experience)
- Career and future goals (dream jobs, future aspirations)
- Social issues (discrimination, education, healthcare, environment)
- Technology and modern life
- Personal growth and development
- Cultural exchange and international perspectives

---

## Content Requirements

### Sentence Length
- **Complex:** 15-20 words average
- **Compound-complex sentences common and expected
- **Multiple clauses:** "When I went to the university, which is located in Osaka, I met a professor who..."

### Line Count per Story
- **Standard:** 10-12 lines
- **Pattern:** 1 emoji + 1 English sentence + 1 Japanese translation

### Question Format
- **3 discussion questions:**
  - At least 1 opinion question (What do you think about...?)
  - At least 1 hypothetical question (What would you do if...?)
  - At least 1 personal experience question
- **Complex answers required:** Questions should require full paragraphs, not 1-2 sentences
- **Critical thinking:** Encourage analysis, evaluation, and synthesis

---

## Special Considerations

### High School Graduate Level
- **Maturity:** Students are 17-18 years old, preparing for university/work
- **Critical thinking:** Questions should require analysis and evaluation
- **Nuanced opinions:** Encourage expressing well-reasoned positions on issues

### Formal/Academic Register
- **Not conversational anymore:** Content should reflect academic/professional contexts
- **Complex ideas:** Topics should be sophisticated, not childish
- **Evidence-based reasoning:** Opinions should be supported by reasons and examples

### Speaking Test Preparation
- **6-7 minute interview:** Eiken 2 has mandatory speaking test
- **Content should prepare students for:** Interview questions, presentation skills, opinion expression

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Grammar
- ❌ Incorrect relative clause structure — verify "who/which/that" used correctly
- ❌ Conditional confusion — especially Type 2 vs Type 3
- ❌ Incorrect modal perfects — "should have done" not "should did"
- ❌ Passive voice errors — especially with complex tenses
- ❌ Too informal register — this level requires formal tone

### Vocabulary
- ❌ Too simple vocabulary — aim for B2 level (5,000-7,500 words)
- ❌ Slang/colloquialisms — maintain formal register
- ❌ Inappropriate topic complexity — topics should be challenging but accessible

### Content
- ❌ Too conversational — maintain academic/professional tone
- ❌ Boring topics — choose engaging, relevant social/academic topics
- ❌ Lacking nuance — questions should explore multiple perspectives

```javascript
    title: "1. Choosing a University Major",
```

### Example 2 (Social Issues - Conditionals)
```javascript
{
    title: "2. Discrimination and Education",
    content: `📖 Education is a fundamental right for everyone.
教育は誰にとっても基本的な権利です。

🚫 Unfortunately, discrimination still exists in many schools.
残念ながら、多くの学校で差別がまだ存在しています。

💡 If we promote diversity, we can create better learning environments.
もしあなた多様性を推進すれば、より良い学習環境を作れます。

🤝 Teachers should ensure equal opportunities for all students.
教師はすべての学生に平等な機会を確保すべきです。

Q: What can schools do to eliminate discrimination?
Q: If you witnessed discrimination, what would you do?

```

### Example 3 (Technology - Complex Modals)
```javascript

AIが人間の労働者を置き換えるのではないかと心配する人もいます。

しかし、AIは人間の能力を高めるツールとして使用すべきです。
```

## Grammar Check for Level 2

Before creating content, verify:
- [ ] Relative clauses used correctly (defining and non-defining)
- [ ] Correct relative pronouns (who, which, that, where, when, why)
- [ ] Conditionals correct (Types 0, 1, 2, 3)
- [ ] Modals in past used correctly (should have done, could have gone)
- [ ] Passive voice used correctly in various tenses
- [ ] Complex sentences flow naturally
- [ ] Vocabulary is from B2 level (5,000-7,500 words)
- [ ] Register is formal/academic, not conversational
- [ ] Questions require complex, analytical answers
- [ ] Topics are age-appropriate for 17-18 year olds

---

## Speaking Test Considerations

Eiken 2 has a 6-7 minute speaking interview with:
- Presentation on given topic
- Q&A session
- Opinion questions on abstract topics

### Students should practice:
- Structured presentations (introduction, main points, conclusion)
- Responding to follow-up questions
- Expressing opinions with reasoning
- Using formal register in spoken English

### Content preparation:
- Include opinion questions that require extended reasoning
- Include hypothetical questions that require conditional usage
- Include questions that allow students to demonstrate complex grammar
