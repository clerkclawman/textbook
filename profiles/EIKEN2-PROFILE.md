# EIKEN 2 (2級) - Reference Profile

**Source:** 2025 EIKEN Test Papers (1st, 2nd, 3rd Sessions)  
**Last Verified:** 2026-03-23  
**Status:** ✅ **Active Reference** (Use for all Eiken 2 content creation)

---

## 1. CEFR Alignment & Role
- **Level:** **B1** (True B1 begins here)
- **Role:** The **"Argumentation & Professional Opinion" Level**. Students express **nuanced opinions**, discuss **abstract/philosophical topics**, handle **complex narratives** (Past Perfect, Type 2 Conditionals), and use **formal/professional register**.

## 2. Carried Over from Pre-2 Plus (Review Only)
*These are assumed known. Do not focus on them as the primary learning goal.*
- **Grammar:** Past Perfect, Type 2 Conditionals (light), Complex Relative Clauses, Formal/Abstract Vocabulary.
- **Vocab:** Formal/academic (society, economy, climate change, policy, research, ethics).
- **Sentence Length:** 12-18 words.

## 3. NEW Grammar Focus (The Core)
*These are the **only** new structures introduced at this level.*

| Grammar Point | Function | Example from 2025 Papers |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Type 2 Conditionals (Mastery)** | **NEW:** Hypothetical/hypothetical present/future (full usage) | "I **would be** delighted **if you could** help..." <br> "I **would have gone** if I **had had** time." |
| **Complex Passive (All Tenses)** | **NEW:** Passive in complex contexts, perfect passive | "The people **could have been** injured." <br> "Advertising **has been controlled** strictly." <br> "He **would have been** in last year's play." |
| **Complex Relative Clauses (Advanced)** | **NEW:** Relative pronouns with complex clauses | "An organization **that helps children**." <br> "The time **at which** they hunt..." <br> "The water **that flows naturally**..." <br> "Events **where** students introduced books..." |
| **Abstract/Philosophical Vocabulary** | **NEW:** Ethics, politics, philosophy, democracy | `politics`, `philosophy`, `duty`, `meaning of life`, `democracy`, `government`, `law`, `argument`, `belief`, `opinion`. |
| **Opinion Expression (Nuanced)** | **NEW:** Justifying opinions with evidence | "The politician's **view on education was**..." <br> "His **never changed** ... **that teachers should be paid more**." |

### ❌ **Strictly Excluded (Do Not Use)**
- **Type 3 Conditionals** (If I had known...) - *Only light exposure.*
- **Subjunctive Mood** (I suggest that he be...) - *Not tested.*
- **Very Long Sentences** (>25 words) - *Keep under 20.*
- **Inversion Structures** (Not only..., Only if...) - *Not tested.*
- **Advanced Passive (Future Perfect)** (will have been done) - *Avoid.*

*Focus on **abstract**, **philosophical**, **professional**, and **societal** topics.*

## 5. Sentence Structure & Length
- **Length:** **14-20 words** average. (Some up to 22 for complex ideas).
- **Structure:** Complex sentences with `who`, `which`, `that`, `where`, `when`, `before`, `after`, `by the time`, `until`, `while`, `although`, `because`, `if`, `when`, `since`, `as`, `and`, `but`, `so`.
  - *Example:* "I **would be** delighted **if you could** help me **solve** this problem." (11 words)
  - *Example:* "An organization **that helps children** is very **important** for society." (10 words)
  - *Example:* "The time **at which** they hunt is **very important** for their **survival**." (13 words)
- **Complexity:** Can combine multiple clauses with relative pronouns and complex time markers.

## 6. Example Sentences (Direct from 2025 Tests)
*These are the exact patterns to emulate.*

**Type 2 Conditionals (Hypothetical):**
> "I **would be** delighted **if you could** help me..."
> "I **would have gone** if I **had had** time."
> "**If** I **were** rich, I **would travel** the world."
> "**If** you **could** change one thing, **what would** it **be**?"

**Complex Passive (All Tenses):**
> "The people **could have been** injured in the car crash."
> "Advertising **has been controlled** strictly in recent years."
> "He **would have been** in last year's play."
> "The time **has been changed** due to unforeseen circumstances."
> "**Has been pointed out that** the mirror test is controversial."

**Complex Relative Clauses:**
> "An organization **that helps children** is what I want to work for."
> "The time **at which** they hunt is very important."
> "The water **that flows naturally** from the top..."
> "A lunchtime event **where** students introduced books to each other."
> "The way **that** people think and live has changed."

**Abstract/Philosophical Topics:**
> "The politician's **view on education was** progressive."
> "The **meaning of life** is a deep question."
> "**Democracy** allows people to choose their leaders."
> "**Philosophy** helps us understand ethics and morality."
> "**Duty** and **responsibility** are important for citizens."

**Opinion Expression (Nuanced):**
> "He never changed his **opinion** **that** teachers **should be paid more**."
> "The politician's **view** was that the **policy** would work."
> "**Arguments** often result from people's **misunderstandings**."
> "**Beliefs** shape our **perspectives** on the world."
> "**Opinions** should be supported by **evidence**."

**Questions (Wh- & Yes/No):**
> "What type of **government** does our country have?"
> "What is the **meaning of life**?"
> "**Why** do people **argue** about **politics**?"
> "**What** would you **do** **if** you **had** **more** **time**?"
> "**How** does **democracy** affect **daily life**?"

## 7. Allowed Patterns Only (Strict Positive Constraints)
*The AI must ONLY use these patterns. If a pattern is not listed below, it is FORBIDDEN.*

### ✅ **ALLOWED Verb Forms**
- **Present Simple:** All base verbs.
- **Past Simple:** All common irregulars.
- **Past Continuous:** `was/were + [verb]-ing`.
- **Past Perfect:** `had + past participle`.
- **Present Perfect:** `have/has + past participle`.
- **Future:** `will`, `going to`, `can`, `could`, `may`, `might`, `would`, `should`, `must`, `have to`, `need to`.
- **Conditional (Type 1 & 2):** `If + Present, will/can` AND `If + Past, would/could/might`.
- **Comparatives/Superlatives:** `more`, `less`, `better`, `worse`, `best`, `worst`, `biggest`, `smallest`, `most`, `least`, `-er`, `-est`.
- **Passive (All Tenses):** `am/is/are/was/were/has been/have been/will be/had been/could have been/would have been/might have been + past participle`.
- **Reported Speech (Complex):** `said that`, `told me`, `asked if`, `explained that`, `mentioned that`, `wondered`, `wanted to know`, `argued that`, `claimed that`, `suggested that`, `recommended that`.
- **Relative Clauses (Advanced):** `who`, `which`, `that`, `whose`, `whom`, `where`, `when`, `at which`.

### ✅ **ALLOWED Question Words**
- `What`, `Where`, `Who`, `When`, `Why`, `How`, `How long`, `How many`, `How often`, `Which`, `Did`, `Was`, `Were`, `Will`, `Can`, `Could`, `Have`, `Has`, `Do`, `Does`, `Should`, `Must`, `Have to`, `Need to`, `Would`, `Could`, `Might`.

### ✅ **ALLOWED Sentence Structures**
- **Simple SVO:** "I have been to Japan."
- **Complex (Relative Clauses):** "The book that I read was interesting."
- **Complex (Time Markers):** "After he had finished, he left."
- **Conditional (Type 1 & 2):** "If you have time, come." / "If I had time, I would come."
- **Passive (All Tenses):** "The road was closed." / "It has been limited." / "It had been done." / "It would have been done."
- **Reported (Complex):** "She said that he wanted to be an actor." / "He argued that the policy should change."
- **Compound:** "I went to the park, and I played soccer."

### ❌ **FORBIDDEN (If seen, the output is INVALID)**
- **Type 3 Conditionals:** `If I had known...`, `I would have gone...` (unless for past hypothesis).
- **Subjunctive Mood:** `If I were`, `I suggest that he be`.
- **Inversion Structures:** `Not only...`, `Only if...`.
- **Very Long Sentences:** >25 words.
- **Slang/Colloquial:** Informal language, contractions (unless natural).

## 8. AI Validation Checklist (Run Before Output)
*If ANY item is unchecked, the content is INVALID.*

- [ ] **Verb Check:** Does every verb match the "ALLOWED Verb Forms" list (Present, Past, Past Continuous, Past Perfect, Present Perfect, Future, Passive (all tenses), Reported, Relative)?
- [ ] **Tense Check:** Is there **NO** Type 3 Conditionals or Subjunctive (unless necessary)?
- [ ] **Conditional Check:** Are conditionals **Type 1 or 2** only (no Type 3)?
- [ ] **Question Check:** Are all questions using `What`, `Where`, `Who`, `When`, `Why`, `How`, `Have`, `Has`, `Did`, `Will`, `Can`, `Should`, `Must`, `Would`, `Could`, `Might`?
- [ ] **Length Check:** Is every sentence **≤ 20 words** (average 14-15)?
- [ ] **Complexity Check:** Are compound/complex sentences using `who`, `which`, `that`, `where`, `when`, `at which`, `before`, `after`, `by the time`, `until`, `while`, `although`, `because`, `if`, `when`, `since`, `as`, `and`, `but`, `so`?
- [ ] **Topic Check:** Is the topic abstract/philosophical (politics, philosophy, duty, meaning of life, democracy, opinion, argument)?
