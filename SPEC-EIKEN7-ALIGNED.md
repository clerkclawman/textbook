### Four Pillars of Grade 7 Success

#### 1. Vocabulary Acquisition (PRIMARY SKILL)

**What the announcement shows:**
- 50-200 target words (much lower than Grade 5's 558)
- Categories: family, animals, colours, numbers, food, toys
- Focus on recognition, NOT production
- Simple, concrete words only (no abstract concepts)

**Teaching implications:**
- Introduce 3-5 new words per story
- Recognise words in context
- Match words to pictures or simple definitions
- NO emphasis on grammatical accuracy

#### 2. Be-Verb Mastery (is/are/am)

**Core forms:**
- "I am..." (first person singular)
- "You are..." (second person)
- "He/She/It is..." (third person singular)
- "We/They are..." (plural)

**Uses:**
- Simple identification: "This is a cat."
- Basic descriptions: "The sky is blue."
- Locations: "My school is here."

**EXCLUDED (based on official announcement):**
- NO Present Continuous ("is jumping", "are playing")
- NO Past Be-verb ("was/were")
- NO questions beyond "Is it...?" or "Are they...?"

#### 3. Present Simple Statements

**Core pattern:** Subject + Verb (s form for third person singular)

**Examples:**
- "I like dogs."
- "She plays games."
- "They walk to school."

**EXCLUDED (based on official announcement):**
- NO Present Continuous ("I am playing")
- NO Modals ("can", "should", "must")
- NO Complex Wh-questions ("Why", "When", "How")
- ONLY basic Yes/No questions: "Is it a dog?", "Do you like cats?"

#### 4. Simple Yes/No Questions

**Question types:**
- Be-verb questions: "Is it red?", "Are they happy?"
- Present Simple DO questions: "Do you like apples?", "Does he play?"

**Question characteristics:**
- One-syllable answers preferred ("Yes", "No", "Cat", "Red")
- NO multi-word sentence answers
- NO inference or complex thinking

### The "Vocabulary NOT Grammar" Principle

**Critical insight from announcement:**

Grade 7's purpose is **vocabulary acquisition**, NOT grammar teaching. The official description explicitly states:

> "Focus: Vocabulary acquisition, NOT grammar"

**Teaching implications:**
- Don't teach grammar rules explicitly
- Students should recognise patterns THROUGH exposure
- Mistakes in grammar are EXPECTED and acceptable
- Prioritise learning new words over grammatical accuracy

**Example story focus:**
- âœ… GOOD: Learn 5 new animal words in a story ("cat", "dog", "bird", "fish", "rabbit")
- âŒ WRONG: Teach Present Simple rule of adding "-s" for third person singular

**Assessment expectation:** Students should recognise and understand words in context. Grammatical accuracy is NOT the primary assessment criterion.
