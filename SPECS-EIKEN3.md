## Grammar Specifications

### Required Grammar Points

#### Present Perfect (Core Focus)
- **Basic form:** have + past participle
- **Experience:** I have been to Tokyo / I have eaten sushi
- **Action with present relevance:** I have lived here for 10 years
- **Questions:** Have you ever...? / Have you been...?

#### Superlatives
- **Regular:** biggest / smallest / tallest / smartest
- **Irregular:** best / worst / farthest
- **In context:** most exciting / most beautiful / most difficult

#### Comparatives (Review + Expansion)
- **Regular comparatives:** more interesting / more expensive
- **"More" structure:** longer than / more beautiful than

#### Conditionals Type 1 (Future Conditional)

#### Future Forms (Review)
- **"Will" + "Going to":** Both acceptable and used appropriately
- **Present continuous for future:** I am meeting him tomorrow

#### Past Continuous
- **Ongoing past actions:** I was playing tennis when it started raining

### Grammar Progression (48 Stories)
- Stories 1-12: Present perfect mastery (Have you ever...?)
- Stories 13-24: Superlatives and comparatives
- Stories 25-36: Conditionals (Type 1)
- Stories 37-48: Mixed tenses + more complex expressions

## Vocabulary Characteristics

### Word Types
- **School vocabulary:** cultural festival, sports day, club activities, exams
- **Hobbies and interests:** music, movies, books, sports, travel, food
- **Descriptive adjectives:** exciting, interesting, beautiful, difficult, expensive, cheap
- **Time expressions:** recently, lately, already, yet, since, for
- **Location vocabulary:** station, hospital, restaurant, park, library
- **Basic emotions:** happy, sad, excited, nervous, surprised, disappointed

### Vocabulary Complexity
- **2-3 syllables:** More complex words allowed
- **Descriptive language:** More adjectives and adverbs
- **Specialized vocabulary:** Topic-specific terms (e.g., cultural festival vocabulary)
- **Abstract concepts:** Beginning of abstract thinking

### Common Topics
- School events (cultural festival, sports day, club activities)
- Hobbies and personal interests (what do you like to do? what are you good at?)
- Travel and experiences (have you ever been to...?)
- Future plans and dreams
- Comparisons and preferences (what is your favorite...? what do you like most...?)
- Conditional situations (if X happens, what will you do?)

---

## Content Requirements

### Sentence Length
- **Moderate to complex:** 10-15 words average
- **Compound sentences common:** "I went to the festival and watched the dance performance"
- **Complex sentences allowed:** "If it rains tomorrow, we will cancel the picnic"

### Line Count per Story
- **Standard:** 10-12 lines
- **Pattern:** 1 emoji + 1 English sentence + 1 Japanese translation

### Question Format
- **3 discussion questions:**
  - At least 1 present perfect question (Have you ever...?)
  - At least 1 comparative/superlative question
  - 1 conditional or opinion question
- **No simple yes/no:** Questions should require full sentences as answers
- **Personal + Opinion:** Questions should combine personal experience with expression of opinion

---

## Special Considerations

### First Mandatory Speaking Level
- **Speaking test required:** 5-minute interview
- **Content should prepare students:** Include "Have you ever...?" questions that simulate speaking test
- **Answer the question, don't just answer:** Encourage extended answers, not just "yes" or "no"
- **Opinion expression:** Questions should ask "What do you think about...?" "Which do you prefer...?"

### Engagement Level
- **Junior high graduates:** 14-15 years old
- **More sophisticated topics:** Not childish, but still age-appropriate
- **Teen interests:** Pop culture, technology, future dreams, social issues (basic)
- **Cultural festivals:** Very relevant to Japanese junior high experience

### Critical Grammar Points
- **Present perfect:** This is the single most important grammar point for Eiken 3
- **Superlatives:** Used frequently in test questions
- **Conditionals:** Foundation for Eiken 2 and beyond

## Grammar Check for Level 3

Before creating content, verify:
- [ ] Present perfect used correctly (have/has + past participle)
- [ ] Have you ever...? questions included
- [ ] Superlatives use "the" correctly
- [ ] Conditionals are Type 1 (If + present, will + base)
- [ ] Not using Type 2 or Type 3 conditionals
- [ ] Comparative/superlative questions included
- [ ] Vocabulary is from core 1,500-2,500 words
- [ ] Questions require extended answers (not just yes/no)
- [ ] Topics are age-appropriate for junior high graduates (14-15 years)
- [ ] Engaging content, not boring drills

---

## Speaking Test Considerations

When creating content, remember that Eiken 3 includes a 5-minute speaking interview:

### Students should practice:
- Answering "Have you ever...?" questions with extended responses
- Expressing opinions with reasons ("I think... because...")
- Comparing and choosing ("I prefer X because...")
- Describing experiences in past and present perfect tenses

### Content preparation:
- Include opinion-focusing questions ("What do you think about...?")
- Include personal experience questions ("Tell me about a time when...")
- Include preference questions ("Which do you prefer...? Why?")
